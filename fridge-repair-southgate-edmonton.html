Common Fridge Faults in Southgate

Frigidaire Defrost System Failure

Frigidaire top-freezer fridges at 18–25 years commonly develop defrost system faults — heater, bi-metal thermostat, or defrost timer. Frost builds on the evaporator coil, blocking airflow and causing the fresh food section to warm. Inexpensive repair, parts typically on-van.

GE Evaporator Fan Motor

GE fridges with a failing evaporator fan make a buzzing or grinding sound from the freezer section. The fan stops circulating cold air, the fridge section warms while the freezer stays cold. Fan motor replacement runs $120–$205 and is typically completed same-day.

Samsung French-Door Ice Maker

Samsung French-door ice makers at 10–15 years develop frost buildup that stops ice production. The correct fix is the Samsung-specified defrost heater upgrade kit — we install this as the permanent solution, not a temporary manual defrost.

Hard Water Inlet Valve

South Edmonton's 170–185 mg/L water hardness scales inlet valve screens, causing slow or absent ice maker fill. We clean or replace the water inlet valve and advise on refrigerator water filter replacement intervals to maintain clean water flow.

Door Gasket Replacement

Worn door gaskets on Frigidaire and GE fridges allow warm air infiltration, causing continuous compressor operation and rising electricity bills. Gasket replacement at $80–$135 is one of the most impactful single-component fridge repairs available.

Defrost Drain Blockage

Water pooling under the fridge or inside the crisper drawers typically means the defrost drain tube is blocked — a common issue in older Frigidaire and GE units. We clear the drain and confirm proper routing. Usually a $75–$110 fix.

FAQ

Fridge Repair Questions — Southgate

Why is my Frigidaire fridge warm but the freezer is cold? +
This pattern almost always means the defrost system has failed — frost has built up on the evaporator coil and is blocking airflow to the fridge section. The freezer stays cold because it's directly connected to the evaporator, but the fridge section can't get enough cold air. Defrost heater, thermostat, or timer replacement resolves it.
How quickly can you come to Southgate? +
Approximately 18 minutes from our Edmonton office. Same-day appointments available most days — book online or email edmonton@fixlifyservices.com.
My Samsung ice maker stopped making ice — is it worth fixing? +
Yes, if the fridge itself is in good working condition. The ice maker frost-over on Samsung French-door models is a known issue with a documented fix — the defrost heater upgrade kit. At $155–$250 installed, it's a cost-effective repair that restores ice production permanently rather than temporarily.
Is it normal to have water under my fridge? +
No. Water under a fridge means either the defrost drain tube is blocked (defrost water overflowing), the drain pan has cracked, or there's a water line issue at the back of the fridge. Blocked defrost drains are by far the most common cause and are a quick, inexpensive fix.

Refrigerators in Edmonton Winters — What Southgate Edmonton Homeowners Need to Know

Edmonton regularly hits −30°C to −40°C in January and February. For refrigerators in garages, unheated mudrooms, or basement utility spaces in Southgate Edmonton, extreme cold creates failure modes that most appliance guides don't cover. Our technicians handle more cold-weather fridge failures per capita than almost anywhere else in Canada.

  • Garage fridges stop cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Southgate Edmonton garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off because the ambient temperature tricks it into thinking the fridge is already cold — your freezer then warms up. A garage kit (available for some Whirlpool and GE models) or an insulated enclosure solves this.
  • Frozen water lines: In-door water dispensers and ice makers in Southgate Edmonton homes with cold setups regularly freeze the fill line during deep cold snaps. We clear the line and reroute if needed to prevent recurrence.
  • Compressor start failures: Cold temperatures thicken compressor oil and increase startup resistance. A start relay on the edge of failure will fail in the first deep freeze of the Edmonton winter. Replacing the relay ($60–$110) preemptively avoids a no-cooling emergency in January.
  • Door seal brittleness: Rubber gaskets harden in cold weather. A brittle gasket in a Southgate Edmonton cold room fails to seal properly, causing the compressor to run continuously and dramatically shortening its life.

If your Southgate Edmonton fridge starts behaving oddly as temperatures drop in October or November, call us before it becomes a full failure — preventive service is far less expensive than an emergency repair in January.
