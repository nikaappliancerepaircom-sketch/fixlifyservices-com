What We Fix

Common Fridge Faults in Pleasantview

Defrost System (Whirlpool / Kenmore)

Defrost heater, bi-metal thermostat, or timer failure causes frost buildup on the evaporator coil, blocking airflow. The fridge section warms while the freezer stays cold. All three components are inexpensive and commonly found on the van for same-day repair.

Ice Maker Module Failure

Older Whirlpool and Kenmore ice maker modules stop cycling when the internal thermostat or motor fails. We assess whether the module is worth replacing given the fridge's age — sometimes the practical answer is disabling the ice maker and using trays.

Evaporator Fan Motor

Fan motor bearing failure causes a grinding or buzzing sound from the freezer section. The cold air circulation stops, and the fridge section warms. Fan motor replacement runs $120–$200 and is completed same-day in most cases.

Door Gasket Degradation

Rubber gaskets on older fridges harden and crack, allowing warm-air infiltration and continuous compressor operation. A $80–$130 gasket replacement is among the most impactful single-part repairs — it also noticeably reduces electricity consumption.

Temperature Control / Thermostat

Mechanical thermostats on older Whirlpool and Kenmore fridges fail in both directions — fridge not cooling, or fridge freezing food. Thermostat replacement is $95–$145 and restores precise temperature control.

Hard Water Inlet Valve

South Edmonton's hard water scales inlet valve screens over time, reducing flow to the ice maker. We clean or replace the valve depending on the scale buildup level, and advise on filter maintenance.

Refrigerators in Edmonton Winters — What Pleasantview Homeowners Need to Know

Edmonton regularly hits −30°C to −40°C in January and February. For refrigerators in garages, unheated mudrooms, or basement utility spaces in Pleasantview, extreme cold creates failure modes that most appliance guides don't cover. Our technicians handle more cold-weather fridge failures per capita than almost anywhere else in Canada.

  • Garage fridges stop cooling: Most refrigerators are designed to operate between 10°C and 43°C. When your Pleasantview garage drops below 10°C, the compressor shuts off because the ambient temperature tricks it into thinking the fridge is already cold — your freezer then warms up. A garage kit (available for some Whirlpool and GE models) or an insulated enclosure solves this.
  • Frozen water lines: In-door water dispensers and ice makers in Pleasantview homes with cold setups regularly freeze the fill line during deep cold snaps. We clear the line and reroute if needed to prevent recurrence.
  • Compressor start failures: Cold temperatures thicken compressor oil and increase startup resistance. A start relay on the edge of failure will fail in the first deep freeze of the Edmonton winter. Replacing the relay ($60–$110) preemptively avoids a no-cooling emergency in January.
  • Door seal brittleness: Rubber gaskets harden in cold weather. A brittle gasket in a Pleasantview cold room fails to seal properly, causing the compressor to run continuously and dramatically shortening its life.

If your Pleasantview fridge starts behaving oddly as temperatures drop in October or November, call us before it becomes a full failure — preventive service is far less expensive than an emergency repair in January.
