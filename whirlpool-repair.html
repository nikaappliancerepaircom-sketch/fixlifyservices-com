Common Whirlpool Appliance Problems We Fix

Our technicians have repaired thousands of Whirlpool appliances across Toronto and the GTA. These are the most frequent faults we encounter:

Washer Not Spinning

Whirlpool top-load washers frequently develop spin failures from worn lid switch actuators, broken drive belts, or failed motor couplings. We stock Whirlpool direct drive components and repair most top-load spin failures in under an hour.

Refrigerator Ice Maker Problems

Whirlpool ice makers fail from frozen fill tubes, faulty water inlet valves, or broken ice maker modules. The Whirlpool icemaker kit is one of the most replaced parts in the industry — we carry it on every van.

F Code Errors (Ovens)

Whirlpool ovens display F1, F2, F3, F5 codes indicating control board, temperature sensor, or door lock failures. Each code maps to a specific component. We diagnose and replace the faulty part rather than defaulting to a full control board replacement.

Dishwasher Not Cleaning Properly

Whirlpool dishwashers lose cleaning performance from clogged spray arms, failing wash pumps, or a malfunctioning water inlet valve delivering insufficient pressure. We clean, repair, or replace these components as needed.

Dryer Thermal Fuse Blown

The thermal fuse is the most-replaced Whirlpool dryer part. A blown fuse means the dryer will not heat or start. We replace the fuse and inspect the exhaust duct blockage that typically caused the overheating in the first place.

Refrigerator Not Cooling

Whirlpool refrigerators develop cooling failures from dirty condenser coils (bottom-freezer models), failed evaporator fans, or defrost system malfunctions. We carry the parts for same-day diagnosis and repair.

Whirlpool Repair FAQ

Do you repair older Whirlpool appliances from the 1990s and 2000s?
Yes. Whirlpool's parts availability is excellent and older models from the 1990s and 2000s are often worth repairing. We source parts from our supplier network and can repair most Whirlpool models regardless of age. Call (437) 524-1053.
My Whirlpool washer agitator is not working. Can you fix it?
Yes. Whirlpool top-load agitator failure is usually a worn agitator directional cogs set or a broken drive block. These are inexpensive parts that take 30 to 45 minutes to replace. We fix agitator faults same-day.
What does the F2 error on my Whirlpool oven mean?
F2 on Whirlpool ovens indicates the oven temperature sensor is reading too high. This is usually a shorted temperature sensor or control board issue. We test both components and replace only what is failing to keep repair costs down.
Is it worth repairing a 10-year-old Whirlpool appliance?
Whirlpool machines are built to last 15 to 20 years with proper maintenance. A 10-year-old Whirlpool is worth repairing unless the repair cost exceeds 60 percent of replacement cost. We give you an honest assessment before starting work.
