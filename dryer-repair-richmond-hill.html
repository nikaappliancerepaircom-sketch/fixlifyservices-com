How We Diagnose Dryer Problems in Richmond Hill

Our technicians are trained specifically for the conditions found in Richmond Hill homes, from affluent suburban homes on large lots, estate properties along Bayview, and modern condo developments near the Yonge corridor. Here is exactly how we isolate the fault:

  1. Lint & Airflow Assessment: Restricted airflow is the root cause of most dryer failures. We start by inspecting the lint trap, the internal lint chute, and the external vent duct from the dryer to the outside wall. A partially blocked vent forces the dryer to overheat, tripping the thermal fuse or burning out the heating element prematurely.
  2. Thermal System Check: We test the heating element (electric) or igniter and gas valve coils (gas) along with the high-limit thermostat, cycling thermostat, and thermal fuse. These components work as a safety chain ‚Äî if one fails, the dryer either will not heat at all or will overheat dangerously. We test each one individually with a multimeter.
  3. Mechanical & Motor Inspection: Drum rollers, idler pulleys, and drive belts wear out over time. A squeaking, thumping, or rattling dryer almost always has a worn drum roller or a fraying belt. We check belt tension, roller bearing play, and motor amperage draw to isolate the noise source precisely.
  4. Control & Sensor Verification: Modern dryers use moisture sensors and electronic control boards to regulate cycle length and temperature. We test sensor bar continuity, board relay outputs, and door switch function. We explain every finding before recommending repair ‚Äî you always know the cost before we start.
