Common Rosedale Gas Stove Issues

Rosedale homeowners most frequently contact us about gas stove ignition irregularities. A Wolf or Viking burner that lights inconsistently — sparking but not catching, or catching briefly before extinguishing — is usually caused by one of three Rosedale-specific factors: incoming gas pressure variation due to long pipe runs from the meter to the kitchen, burner port blockage from renovation dust that was not fully cleaned after the kitchen installation, or a gas regulator that was sized for the home's original gas load and has not been upgraded to accommodate the higher BTU demand of a premium range. Our technicians test gas pressure at the stove connection with a manometer before touching any stove components — this 60-second test either confirms adequate supply or identifies the root cause before any unnecessary parts are replaced.

Temperature sensor calibration is the second most common Rosedale service request. Rosedale homeowners who invest in premium gas ranges expect precise temperature performance, and a 20-degree variance that might go unnoticed in a standard kitchen is immediately apparent to someone who paid $15,000 for their range. We calibrate the oven temperature sensor against a reference thermometer placed at three positions in the cavity — centre rack, upper third, and lower third — and adjust the offset in the control board until all three readings fall within 5 degrees of the set temperature.
