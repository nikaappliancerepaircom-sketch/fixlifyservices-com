Water Quality and Laundry Performance in Lawrence Park

Lawrence Park residents are attentive to laundry quality — whites that are truly white, colours that retain their vibrancy, and delicates that maintain their texture. Toronto's hard water works against these goals: mineral deposits make fabrics feel stiff, whites develop a grey tinge over time, and detergent doesn't dissolve fully at the 115 to 140 mg/L hardness level of Toronto municipal water. Many Lawrence Park homes have whole-house water softeners that address this issue, but the softener itself can affect the washer if the softener salt level drops and the system starts passing hard water through. Our technicians check the water quality at the washer supply and advise on softener maintenance when hard-water symptoms appear.

Flood protection is a priority in Lawrence Park laundry rooms, especially when the laundry is on the second floor above finished living spaces. Premium washers include flood-stop systems — automatic shutoff valves that detect leaks and close the water supply. When these systems malfunction, the washer refuses to fill and displays an error code. Our technicians test and reset flood-stop systems, and when necessary, replace the electronic shutoff valve to restore normal operation.
