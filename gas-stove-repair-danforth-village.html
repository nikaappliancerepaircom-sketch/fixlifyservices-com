Danforth Village Gas Stove — Common Repair Scenarios

The most frequent Danforth Village gas stove call is the continuously clicking burner. The homeowner turns the knob, the igniter clicks rapidly, but the burner does not light — or it lights briefly and goes out. In Danforth kitchens where stovetop cooking generates heavy steam from boiling pasta water and roasting juices, moisture infiltrates the igniter switch module beneath the cooktop surface. The switch contacts corrode or develop a moisture film that prevents clean electrical contact. We disassemble the burner assembly, clean the switch contacts, dry the module housing, and test each burner for reliable ignition across all flame settings. If the switch module is corroded beyond cleaning — which happens in kitchens with poor ventilation above the stove — we replace the entire igniter switch assembly.

Oven temperature inconsistency is the second most common Danforth Village complaint. Greek baking requires precise, sustained oven temperatures — a moussaka that bakes at 375 degrees for 45 minutes cannot tolerate a 30-degree temperature swing mid-cycle without affecting the bechamel topping. Gas oven temperature accuracy depends on the interplay between the igniter, the gas safety valve, and the temperature sensor. When the igniter weakens, the valve opens later in each heating cycle, and the oven undershoots the target temperature. When the temperature sensor drifts, the control board receives incorrect cavity temperature readings and cycles the burner at the wrong intervals. How does Fixlify Appliance Services diagnose a gas stove oven that does not heat?+

We start with igniter amperage testing using a clamp meter on the igniter lead wire. A healthy igniter draws 3.2 to 3.6 amps. Below 3.0 amps, the igniter cannot open the gas safety valve even though it may appear to glow normally. This single test identifies the root cause in over 80 percent of gas stove oven no-heat complaints without unnecessary gas valve replacement.
