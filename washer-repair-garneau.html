Common Garneau Washer Problems

The washer faults we diagnose most frequently in Garneau, weighted toward the Whirlpool, GE, Samsung, LG, heavy-use machines appliance profile.

Not draining / error code
Drain pump failure or clogged pump filter. Samsung 5E / LG OE codes indicate drain fault. Same-day fix in most cases.
Drum bearing noise (front-load)
Grinding or rumbling noise on spin cycle — bearing wear in front-loaders 8–14 years old. We carry bearing kits for major brands.
Door boot seal leak
Boot seal tears cause floor leaks. OEM seal replacement plus mould-prevention walkthrough on every call.
Lid switch / door latch
Lid switch failure prevents spin (top-load). Door latch fault prevents cycle start (front-load). Standard same-day repair.
Inlet valve / no water fill
Hard water scales inlet valve screens over 6–8 years. 4E/4C under-fill errors on Samsung and LG. Descale + valve replacement.
Excessive vibration
Worn shock absorbers or out-of-balance issue. Front-loaders on hard flooring especially prone to vibration damage.

Washer Care Tips for Garneau Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. A neglected one typically fails at 7–9 years. Edmonton's hard water and cold winters add extra stress on washing machines in Garneau homes — here is how to extend your washer's life based on the repairs we see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh works well in Edmonton's hard water). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration start.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Edmonton's hard water can cause slow leaks at the connection point that go undetected for months before causing floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Garneau homes.
  • Winter tip: If your washer is in an unheated space in Garneau, do not leave damp laundry in the drum when temperatures drop below −5°C — frozen residual water in the pump can cause pump failure on the next cycle.
