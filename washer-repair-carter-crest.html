Common Washer Repair Problems We Fix in Carter Crest

The most common washer faults we diagnose in Carter Crest, based on our service history across SW Edmonton's 1990s residential neighbourhoods.

Not spinning / drum stuck
Drive belt or coupling failure on top-load units — very common in 1990s Carter Crest Kenmore and Maytag washers. OEM belts and couplings on the truck for same-day replacement.
Water not draining
Drain pump failure or kinked drain hose. Clothes sit in standing water at cycle end. Most common in top-load units 15+ years old across Carter Crest homes.
Front-load door seal leak
Front-load washer door boot seals degrade after 8–12 years, especially in hard-water areas. Water leaks onto flooring during cycles. OEM door boot seals replace the gasket and restore watertight operation.
Bearing noise / loud during spin
Drum bearings wear out on front-load washers, producing loud rumbling or grinding during spin. Bearing replacement is a major repair — we assess cost vs. replacement and advise honestly.
Samsung / LG error codes
Samsung UE, 5E, and E3 error codes on front-load washers indicate drum imbalance, drain faults, and door lock failures. LG OE and UE codes follow similar patterns. We diagnose and repair same-day.
Control board / lid switch failure
Top-load washers with failed lid switches won't agitate or spin. Front-load control boards develop capacitor failures causing error codes and stuck cycles. Both are same-day repairs with OEM parts.
