Maytag Washer Repair — Common Issues We Fix

Our technicians have extensive experience with Maytag washer repair faults. We diagnose accurately and repair on the first visit in most cases.

Washer Not Draining

Maytag top-load washers develop drain pump clogging from lint and foreign objects, and front-load models show F21 error codes indicating slow drain. We clear pump filters and replace drain pumps as needed.

Dishwasher Not Cleaning

Maytag dishwashers lose cleaning effectiveness from worn spray arm bearings, failing wash pumps, or a faulty water inlet valve. We inspect the full wash system and replace worn components.

Washer Agitator Not Working

Maytag top-load agitator washers develop agitator directional cog failures — a common and inexpensive repair. If the drum spins but the agitator does not move, the cogs need replacement.

Maytag Washer Repair in Toronto — Tough Built, Easy to Fix

Maytag washers are marketed as "built to last" — and in our Toronto service data, they generally live up to it, averaging 10–12 years before first significant repair. The MVW7230 top-load and MHW5630 front-load models are our most frequently serviced Maytag washers in the GTA, typically needing repair only after 7–9 years of heavy household use. Maytag's modular component design means most repairs are straightforward: drive belts, agitator assemblies, lid locks, and pump motors replace without specialized tooling, and OEM Maytag parts are readily available from Toronto suppliers.

The F9 E1 drain error is the most common Maytag washer fault code in Toronto. Maytag top-load washers discharge through a drain pump that can accumulate lint, hair, and small items (coins are the primary culprit in models without a coin trap). When the impeller becomes blocked, the pump runs but cannot move water, triggering F9 E1. Our technicians access the pump from the front or bottom panel and clear blockages without disassembling the drum or top panel. In cases where the pump motor has failed from continuous blocked-filter operation, OEM Maytag pump assemblies install in about 40 minutes at $55–$80 in parts cost.

Maytag top-load washers (MVW7232, MVWB765 series) use a Maytag-exclusive PowerWash system that combines hot water, high spin speeds, and concentrated detergent targeting. The PowerWash agitator assembly uses a deep-roll impeller that generates stronger currents than conventional agitators — but the impeller fins develop stress fractures at the fin-to-hub junction after heavy use with large loads. When the fins break, the washer makes a grinding noise during agitation cycles. Fixlify technicians replace Maytag PowerWash impeller assemblies routinely — the repair typically completes in 45 minutes and costs $70–$110 in OEM parts, restoring full PowerWash performance.
