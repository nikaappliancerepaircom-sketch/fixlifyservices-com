Professional Refrigerator Repair Across Toronto & the GTA

Your refrigerator runs around the clock, keeping food safe and fresh for your household. When it stops cooling properly, leaks water onto the kitchen floor, or makes grinding noises at 3 a.m., the clock starts ticking on spoiled groceries and growing frustration. Fixlify Appliance Services delivers fast, reliable fridge repair to every neighbourhood across Toronto and the Greater Toronto Area, from downtown condos on Queen Street to suburban homes in Markham and Oakville.

Refrigerators are the most mechanically complex kitchen appliance, combining sealed refrigerant systems, precision thermostats, electronic control boards, defrost heaters, evaporator fans, and water supply lines for ice makers. Each component can fail independently, and diagnosing the root cause requires hands-on experience with specific brand architectures. Our technicians carry diagnostic meters, refrigerant gauges, and the most commonly needed parts on every service call so that the majority of fridge repairs are completed in a single visit without a follow-up appointment.

Common Refrigerator Problems We Repair

The issues we see most frequently across GTA homes fall into predictable categories. A fridge that stops cooling is often traced to a failed compressor relay, a clogged condenser coil, or a malfunctioning defrost system that causes ice to block the evaporator. Water pooling inside the unit or on the floor typically points to a blocked drain tube or a cracked water inlet valve. Ice maker failures are extremely common in Samsung and LG French-door models, where the ice maker assembly or water line freezes solid during winter months when household temperatures drop.

  • Not Cooling or Warm Fridge: Compressor relay, condenser fan, thermostat, or sealed system issue. Cost: $160-$400.
  • Water Leaking Inside or Outside: Blocked defrost drain, cracked drain pan, or failed water inlet valve. Cost: $100-$220.
  • Ice Maker Not Working: Frozen water line, faulty ice maker module, or defective water inlet valve. Cost: $130-$280.
  • Fridge Running Constantly: Dirty condenser coils, worn door gaskets, or faulty temperature sensor. Cost: $90-$250.
  • Strange Noises (Buzzing, Clicking, Grinding): Failing evaporator fan motor, condenser fan motor, or compressor. Cost: $120-$350.
  • Frost Buildup in Freezer: Defrost heater, defrost thermostat, or defrost timer/control board failure. Cost: $150-$300.

When to Repair vs. Replace Your Fridge

A well-maintained refrigerator lasts 12-18 years on average. If your unit is under 10 years old and the repair cost is less than half the price of a comparable new model, repair is almost always the better financial decision. Our technicians will give you an honest recommendation based on the age and condition of your appliance, never pushing unnecessary replacements.
