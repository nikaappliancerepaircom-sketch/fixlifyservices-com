Professional Stove & Cooktop Repair Across Toronto & the GTA

The stovetop is where most cooking actually happens. From boiling pasta to searing steaks to simmering sauces, your stove handles more daily use than almost any other appliance in the kitchen. When a burner refuses to light, an electric element stays cold, or a cracked glass cooktop becomes a safety hazard, meal preparation grinds to a halt. Fixlify Appliance Services delivers fast, dependable stove and cooktop repair to homes across Toronto and the Greater Toronto Area, from highrise condos downtown to family kitchens in Vaughan and Ajax.

Today's stovetops come in three fundamentally different technologies: gas, radiant electric, and induction. Each uses entirely different heating methods and failure modes. Gas stoves rely on spark ignition modules, burner valve assemblies, and flame supervision devices. Electric cooktops use resistance coil elements or radiant ribbon elements beneath ceramic glass. Induction cooktops generate heat through electromagnetic coils that interact with ferrous cookware and require specialized inverter board diagnostics. Our licensed technicians are trained across all three platforms and arrive with the meters, test equipment, and parts needed to handle the vast majority of stove repairs in a single appointment.

Common Stove Problems We Repair

Stovetop issues often present themselves clearly but can stem from multiple underlying causes. A gas burner that clicks repeatedly but never ignites may have a clogged igniter port, a cracked spark electrode, or a faulty ignition switch. An electric element that heats intermittently could be caused by a burned-out element, a loose terminal connection, or a failing infinite switch. Induction cooktops that display error codes frequently have overheating inverter boards or cracked sensor zones beneath the glass.

  • Gas Burner Not Lighting: Clogged igniter port, cracked spark electrode, faulty ignition switch, or gas valve issue. Cost: $130-$260.
  • Electric Element Not Heating: Burned-out coil element, failed infinite switch, loose wiring connection, or tripped thermal limiter. Cost: $120-$250.
  • Cracked Glass Cooktop: Impact damage or thermal shock to ceramic glass surface requiring panel replacement. Cost: $200-$380.
  • Gas Smell When Stove Is Off: Leaking gas valve, damaged supply line connection, or worn burner seal. Cost: $150-$300.
  • Control Panel Not Responding: Failed touch control board, blown fuse, or membrane switch wear. Cost: $180-$350.
  • Induction Cooktop Error Codes: Overheating inverter board, cracked sensor zone, or incompatible cookware detection fault. Cost: $200-$380.

Gas Stove Safety in Ontario

Gas stovetop repairs require TSSA-compliant procedures to prevent gas leaks and ensure safe operation. Our technicians carry gas leak detectors and follow all Ontario safety protocols when servicing gas valves, supply connections, and burner assemblies. If you detect a gas odour near your stove, turn off the appliance, open windows, and call us right away. We expedite gas-related service calls and can typically dispatch a technician within hours.

When to Repair vs. Replace Your Stove

A well-built stovetop or range typically lasts 13-17 years depending on brand and usage. If your stove is under 10 years old and the repair estimate falls below half the price of a comparable new model, repair is almost always the better investment. We provide honest, no-pressure assessments focused on what actually makes financial sense for your household.
