Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Belmead

Frequent refrigerator faults in Belmead's 1970s–80s homes and modern renovated kitchens.

Defrost Timer

Defrost System Failure

Mechanical defrost timers in vintage Whirlpool and Kenmore units fail after 15–20 years, causing frost buildup on evaporator coils, reduced airflow, and a warm fresh-food compartment. We replace timers and bimetal thermostats together to restore full cooling.

Compressor Relay

Compressor Start Relay Failure

The start relay on older GE and Kenmore compressors rattles or fails completely, preventing the compressor from starting. You'll hear clicking every few minutes or silence. We test, replace the relay, and verify compressor operation before leaving.

Samsung FF

Ice Maker & Water Dispenser Faults

Samsung French-door and side-by-side fridges in renovated Belmead homes display FF error codes related to ice maker fan or inlet valve failures. We carry Samsung-compatible ice maker assemblies, water inlet valves, and dispenser control boards for fast repair.

Door Gasket

Door Gasket Deterioration

Original door gaskets on 1970s–80s Whirlpool and GE units harden, crack, and lose their seal — allowing warm air infiltration that forces the compressor to run continuously. We supply era-specific replacement gaskets and fit them to factory tolerance.

Evap Fan

Evaporator Fan Motor Seizure

Evaporator fan motors in older top-freezer fridges seize when frost accumulates on the motor shaft, stopping cold-air circulation entirely. Both compartments warm up. We replace the motor, clear frost accumulation, and verify the defrost system is functioning to prevent recurrence.

Thermostat

Temperature Control Thermostat Failure

Mechanical cold-control thermostats in older Kenmore and GE fridges lose calibration or fail open, causing the compressor to run non-stop or not at all. We replace the cold-control, test temperature stability across both compartments, and verify the unit cycles correctly.

Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Belmead

Refrigerator repair calls in Belmead peak in two seasons: summer, when compressors work harder against the heat, and late fall, when Edmonton's temperature drops rapidly and garage fridges begin cycling abnormally. These are the faults our technicians encounter most often in Belmead:

  • →Fridge not cooling or not cold enough — compressor, thermostat, evaporator fan, or condenser coil failure. The evaporator fan is the most common single-point failure on modern frost-free units in Belmead.
  • →Ice building up on the back wall — a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator. This blocks airflow and the fridge gradually warms up. Very common in Belmead during winter months.
  • →Water dispenser or ice maker not working — a frozen fill line or failed water inlet valve. Edmonton homes with in-door dispensers see frozen lines regularly during the coldest weeks of winter.
  • →Fridge running constantly or spiking energy bill — a worn door gasket lets cold air escape and forces the compressor to run continuously. Test: close the door on a sheet of paper — if it slides out easily, the gasket is failing.
  • →Clicking or not starting — a failing start relay is an inexpensive fix ($60–$110) that prevents a full compressor replacement ($450–$750). Catching it early saves significant money.
