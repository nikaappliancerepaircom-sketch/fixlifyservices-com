Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Clareview

These are the faults we diagnose most often in Clareview’s 1970s multi-family and single-family stock — weighted toward Whirlpool, Kenmore, and LG given the neighbourhood’s appliance profile.

Hard water scale build-up
EPCOR’s 170–200 mg/L hardness scales spray arms and inlet valves. Dishes come out cloudy and spotty. We descale and restore spray pressure on-site.
Not draining / standing water
Drain pump failure or clogged drain hose. Common in 10–15 year old Whirlpool and LG units across Clareview apartment suites. Same-day fix in most cases.
Not cleaning dishes
Scaled spray arm nozzles, weak circulation pump, or failed water inlet valve. Hard water accelerates all three faults in Clareview units.
Not starting / no power
Door latch micro-switch, thermal fuse, or control board. Common on older Kenmore built-under models and LG front-panel units installed in 2000s renovations.
Leaking from door or base
Door gasket failure or sump seal leak. We pressure-test the door seal and replace OEM. Frequently seen on Whirlpool WDF and GE units past 10 years.
Heating element failure
Dishes wet at end of cycle, water not reaching wash temperature. Element replacement is a 45-minute same-day fix on most Clareview appliance models.
