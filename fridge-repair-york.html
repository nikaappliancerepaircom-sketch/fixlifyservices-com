Common Fridge Problems We Fix in York

Refrigerator repair calls in York peak in two seasons: summer, when compressors work harder against the heat, and late fall, when Edmonton's temperature drops rapidly and garage fridges begin cycling abnormally. These are the faults our technicians encounter most often in York:

  • →Fridge not cooling or not cold enough — compressor, thermostat, evaporator fan, or condenser coil failure. The evaporator fan is the most common single-point failure on modern frost-free units in York.
  • →Ice building up on the back wall — a failed defrost heater or defrost thermostat causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator. This blocks airflow and the fridge gradually warms up. Very common in York during winter months.
  • →Water dispenser or ice maker not working — a frozen fill line or failed water inlet valve. Edmonton homes with in-door dispensers see frozen lines regularly during the coldest weeks of winter.
  • →Fridge running constantly or spiking energy bill — a worn door gasket lets cold air escape and forces the compressor to run continuously. Test: close the door on a sheet of paper — if it slides out easily, the gasket is failing.
  • →Clicking or not starting — a failing start relay is an inexpensive fix ($60–$110) that prevents a full compressor replacement ($450–$750). Catching it early saves significant money.

A non-functioning fridge is a food safety emergency. We prioritize same-day service for fridge calls in York — book before noon and we aim to arrive that afternoon.
