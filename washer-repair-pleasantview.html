Common Washer Faults in Pleasantview

Whirlpool / Kenmore Top-Load Agitator

Worn agitator dogs — the plastic ratchet tabs that let the agitator move in one direction — are the most common failure on 1990s–2000s Whirlpool and Kenmore washers. A $40–$60 part, straightforward to replace, often restores years of service.

GE Top-Load Clutch Failure

GE top-loaders rely on a brake/clutch assembly to transition between wash and spin. When it wears out, the machine fills and agitates but won't spin. Repair typically runs $130–$190 and restores full function.

Samsung Front-Load Door Lock

Samsung front-loads with dC, dE, or door lock error codes often need just a new door latch assembly. The machine refuses to start as a safety measure. Quick diagnosis and usually a same-day fix.

LG Front-Load Drum Bearing

LG front-loaders are prone to rear bearing wear at 8–12 years, presenting as a loud grinding or roaring noise during spin. We assess whether the repair is cost-effective before proceeding and give a clear recommendation.

Water Not Filling (Inlet Valve)

If the drum fills slowly or not at all, the water inlet valve is typically at fault — often partially blocked with mineral scale from Pleasantview's hard water. Replacement is $100–$160 and usually fixes the issue completely.

Door Boot Seal Mould (Front-Load)

Front-load washers in finished basements — common in Pleasantview renovations — trap moisture inside the door seal fold, promoting mould growth and eventual seal failure. We replace the seal and advise on proper post-wash door-ajar practice.

Common Washer Problems We Repair in Pleasantview

Washing machines in Pleasantview face a combination of regular wear and the particular demands of Edmonton's climate — hard water mineral buildup at 170–200 mg/L, temperature cycling in unheated utility rooms, and the heavy usage patterns of Edmonton households. These are the washer faults our technicians see most often:

  • →Drum not spinning — a failed lid switch (top-loaders) or door latch (front-loaders) prevents the spin cycle from engaging. Common on HE washers over 5 years old.
  • →Excessive vibration or grinding during spin — worn drum bearings are the usual cause. Left untreated, a failing bearing destroys the drum shaft, dramatically increasing repair costs. Call us at the first grinding sound.
  • →Water not draining after cycle — a blocked pump filter or failed drain pump. Front-loaders are particularly prone to small-item blockages that go undetected in Pleasantview homes.
  • →Leaking water — door boot seals on front-loaders and hose connections at the back of the unit are the most common leak points. Edmonton's hard water accelerates seal deterioration.
  • →F21, E3, or UL error codes — these indicate specific component failures that require proper diagnosis. We clear codes with a definitive repair, not a reset workaround.

Most Pleasantview washer repairs are resolved in one visit. We stock drum bearings, pump assemblies, door boot seals, lid switches, and control boards for all major platforms in our service vehicles.
