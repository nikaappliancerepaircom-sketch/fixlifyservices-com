What Is the Actual Difference Between OEM and Aftermarket Parts?

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) parts are identical to the components installed at the factory. They are made by the same supplier that manufactured the original part, or by the appliance brand's in-house production. When you order a Bosch drain pump with part number 00145038, you receive exactly the same component Bosch installs on the production line.

Aftermarket parts are manufactured by independent companies using the OEM part's published specifications as a reference — but without access to the original tooling, material specs, or manufacturing quality controls. The result varies enormously: some aftermarket manufacturers (Supco, Exact Replacements, OEM equivalent) produce parts that are functionally indistinguishable from OEM. Others produce parts with incorrect dimensions, different electrical tolerances, or substandard materials that fail prematurely.

The problem is that the quality of an aftermarket part is not visible from the outside. Two drain pumps may look identical but use different impeller materials, motor coil windings, or bearing grades — differences that only emerge after 6–18 months of operation.

When Fixlify Specifies OEM Parts

Based on nine years of repair data, our technicians always specify OEM for the following component categories:

  • Electronic control boards — aftermarket boards frequently use lower-grade capacitors and relay contacts that fail within 12–18 months. OEM boards carry manufacturer warranties of 90 days to 12 months.
  • Water inlet valves — solenoid coil winding tolerance is critical. Aftermarket valves may draw incorrect current, causing control board relay overloading over time.
  • Inverter motor stators (LG, Samsung) — winding resistance and insulation class must be exact. An incorrect stator damages the inverter board within months.
  • Refrigerator compressors and sealed system components — must meet the refrigerant and lubrication specifications of the original design.
  • Bosch and Miele drain pumps — the European BSH pump design has tight housing tolerances; aftermarket pumps frequently develop seal leaks at the housing joint.

When Quality Aftermarket Parts Are Acceptable

For lower-precision mechanical components where dimensional tolerances are wide, reputable aftermarket parts deliver equivalent performance at meaningful cost savings:

  • Dryer drum belts — generic belts meeting the original dimensions (width, length, rib count) perform identically to OEM. Savings: $15–$25 per belt.
  • Door gaskets (refrigerator and dishwasher) — aftermarket gaskets from quality suppliers match the profile adequately. The gasket material (EPDM rubber) is standardized.
  • Dryer drum rollers — aftermarket rollers with the correct shaft diameter and roller compound work well. Savings: $20–$40 per roller set.
  • Basic dryer igniters (gas range) — commodity ceramic-tip igniters with correct resistance range perform well in most applications.
  • Thermal fuses and basic thermostats — standard electrical components with published specifications; reputable aftermarket versions meet the spec reliably.

Counterfeit "OEM" parts are a real problem in the Toronto appliance parts market. Parts sold on Amazon Marketplace, Kijiji, and low-cost e-commerce sites are sometimes labeled as OEM but are actually low-grade aftermarket components in OEM-style packaging. Our technicians verify part authenticity through authorized Canadian distributor channels — Reliable Parts, Encompass Parts, and manufacturer direct portals. If a part is priced 40%+ below normal OEM retail, it is likely counterfeit.

How to Find the Right Part Number for Your Appliance

1

Locate the Model and Serial Number

Every appliance has a model label: inside the refrigerator door frame, inside the washer door opening, on the back or bottom of dishwashers, and on the back panel of dryers. Note both model and serial number — the serial number identifies the production date and manufacturing batch, which is important for appliances with mid-run design changes.

2

Use Manufacturer Parts Diagrams

LG, Samsung, Whirlpool, Bosch, and GE all publish parts diagrams and part numbers on their service portals. Enter the model number to see an exploded parts diagram with every component's individual part number. This eliminates guesswork about part compatibility.

3

Verify Cross-Reference Numbers

Many components are shared across model lines and branded differently. For example, Kenmore models share Whirlpool parts — the Kenmore part number and Whirlpool WP part number reference the same physical component. Authorized parts suppliers provide cross-reference lookup to confirm compatibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I find appliance parts in Toronto?
Toronto has several appliance parts suppliers including Reliable Parts (multiple locations), Jack's Appliance Parts, and online suppliers like repairclinic.com and appliancepartspros.com that ship to Canadian addresses. Fixlify sources OEM parts directly from manufacturer distributors and maintains a parts inventory for common brands including LG, Samsung, Whirlpool, Bosch, and GE.
Are OEM appliance parts always better than aftermarket?
OEM parts are always the correct specification — they are identical to the factory-installed component. Quality aftermarket parts from reputable manufacturers (Supco, Exact Replacements, WP) are acceptable for low-precision components like door gaskets, knobs, and dryer belts. For precision electronics, solenoids, motors, and control boards, OEM is strongly preferred because dimensional and electrical tolerances are critical and aftermarket versions vary in quality.
Does using aftermarket parts void my appliance warranty?
In Canada, under the Competition Act, manufacturers cannot void your warranty solely because you used a non-OEM part for a repair — unless they can prove the aftermarket part caused the failure. However, service technicians engaged by the manufacturer for warranty work are required to use OEM parts. If your appliance is still under factory warranty, have warranty repairs performed by an authorized service centre.
How much do OEM appliance parts cost compared to aftermarket in Toronto?
OEM parts typically cost 30–60% more than comparable aftermarket parts. For example, a Bosch drain pump (OEM) costs approximately $120–$150 CAD; an aftermarket equivalent runs $60–$90. For short-lived components like door gaskets and belts, aftermarket savings are worthwhile. For expensive electronics and motors, the OEM durability and manufacturer warranty (often 12 months) justify the premium.
