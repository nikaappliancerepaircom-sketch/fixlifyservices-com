How Dishwasher Spray Arms Work

Most dishwashers have two or three spray arms: a lower arm beneath the bottom rack, an upper arm beneath the top rack, and in three-level machines, a middle arm. The circulation pump pressurizes water to 8–12 PSI and routes it through a manifold to each arm. As water exits the angled nozzles, the reaction force causes the arm to rotate — a self-propelled mechanism that requires no motor of its own.

Each arm has between 8 and 20 individual nozzle holes, typically 1.5–3 mm in diameter. When even a few holes are blocked by mineral scale or food debris, the spray pattern becomes uneven, leaving food residue on dishes in the zones covered by the blocked nozzles. When the arm stops rotating entirely due to a jammed hub bearing or physical obstruction, an entire rack may receive no cleaning action.

How to Identify a Blocked vs. Broken Spray Arm

The diagnostic distinction matters because the solutions are different. Our technician Alex M. uses the following approach:

  • Blocked (cleanable): Arm spins freely by hand, but holding it up to light reveals dark or mineral-filled nozzle holes. Cleaning with vinegar soak and toothpick clears the blockage.
  • Jammed hub (mechanical fault): Arm cannot rotate freely by hand due to food debris compacted in the center hub bearing. Hub is cleared and tested for bearing wear — worn bearings require full arm replacement.
  • Cracked arm (replacement required): Hairline cracks along the arm body allow pressurized water to escape before reaching nozzles, reducing spray force. A cracked arm may still rotate but produces weak, misdirected spray.
  • Low pressure (pump fault): Both arms spin but dishes remain dirty across all zones, indicating the circulation pump is not generating sufficient pressure. This requires pump testing, not arm replacement.

Tall utensils can physically jam spray arms mid-cycle. A spatula or long knife protruding below the bottom rack will contact the lower spray arm, stopping rotation and in some cases cracking the arm on impact. Always ensure utensils are fully seated within rack tines. If you notice an unusual noise during a cycle, stop the machine and check for obstructions before damage occurs.

Spray Arm Diagnosis Steps

1

Visual Nozzle and Rotation Inspection

Arms removed, nozzle holes inspected under light for blockage. Arms spun by hand to test hub bearing smoothness. Any roughness, grinding, or restricted rotation in the hub indicates mechanical wear beyond cleaning.

2

Crack and Structural Integrity Check

Each arm body inspected for hairline fractures along the top and underside surfaces. Cracks are often invisible when dry but can be confirmed by filling the arm with water and observing for leaks at pressure.

3

Water Pressure Verification at Arm Port

Technician runs a short wash cycle and places a pressure gauge at the arm supply port. Normal pressure range: 8–12 PSI. Values below 6 PSI indicate a circulation pump or filter issue rather than an arm defect.

4

Filter and Sump Inspection

Dishwasher filter and sump are inspected for accumulated food debris that would restrict pump inlet flow. A clogged filter is cleaned during the same visit — this alone resolves many "dirty dishes" complaints without any part replacement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are my dishes still dirty after a full dishwasher cycle?
Dirty dishes after a complete wash cycle most commonly indicate blocked spray arm holes, a spray arm that isn't rotating due to food debris jamming the bearing hub, or a cracked arm that leaks pressure before water reaches the nozzles. Low water pressure from a clogged filter or faulty circulation pump can also reduce spray effectiveness.
Can I clean dishwasher spray arms myself?
Yes — removing and soaking the arms in warm vinegar solution to dissolve mineral deposits is a safe homeowner task. Use a toothpick to clear individual nozzle holes. However, if an arm is cracked, warped, or its bearing hub is stripped, replacement is needed and the part must be an OEM match to ensure correct spray pattern and rotation.
How much does dishwasher spray arm replacement cost in Toronto?
A single spray arm replacement (lower or upper) runs $85–$150 CAD including labour. Replacing both arms plus a filter service is typically $150–$220. Fixlify provides a digital itemized quote before authorizing any repair.
What causes spray arms to crack or break?
Plastic spray arm fatigue from repeated thermal cycling (cold fill, hot wash, hot dry) causes micro-fractures over time. Physical impact from tall utensils contacting the rotating arm can cause immediate cracking. Hard water mineral buildup gradually weakens nozzle edges. Bosch, LG, and Samsung use more durable arm materials than some budget brands, but all eventually require replacement after 6–10 years of daily use.
