How Edmonton Appliance Repair Pricing Works

Appliance repair companies in Edmonton typically charge in one of two ways: a flat diagnostic fee plus a parts-and-labour quote, or an hourly rate. At Fixlify Edmonton, we use the flat diagnostic model because it gives you a clear picture of costs before committing to any work. The diagnostic fee covers the technician's time to identify the fault and provide a written quote. If you approve the repair, the diagnostic fee is waived — you pay only for parts and labour.

Parts pricing in Alberta reflects Canada-wide OEM supply chain costs. Most appliance manufacturers distribute through regional warehouses in Calgary and Edmonton, so OEM part availability in Edmonton is generally good. Lead times of 2–5 business days apply for less-common parts, though we carry the highest-turnover components on our service vehicles for same-day repairs.

Repair or Replace? The Edmonton Homeowner’s Rule

The 50% rule: if repair cost exceeds 50% of the replacement cost of an equivalent new appliance, replacement is likely the better financial choice. In 2026, new mid-range appliance prices in Edmonton (with delivery and installation from local retailers like Trail Appliances or AppliancesCanada) typically run: fridge $1,200–$2,400, washer $700–$1,400, dryer $600–$1,200, dishwasher $600–$1,400, range $800–$2,000.

A $200 drain pump repair on a 4-year-old dishwasher? Repair. A $700 compressor replacement on a 12-year-old basic fridge? Replace. A $150 heating element job on a 6-year-old LG electric range? Repair every time. Our technicians always give you an honest comparison before any work begins.
