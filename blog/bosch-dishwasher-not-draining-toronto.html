Understanding Bosch Error Codes E24 and E25

Bosch dishwashers use specific error codes to communicate drainage problems:

  • E24: Water is not draining — pump, hose, or filter blockage
  • E25: Drain pump is blocked or the filter mesh is clogged with debris
  • E17: Too much water in the machine (often caused by partial drainage failure)

Both E24 and E25 require the same investigation: filter, pump, and drain hose. Do not continue running cycles when these codes appear — it stresses the drain pump motor and can cause a more expensive secondary failure.

The 5 Causes of Bosch Dishwasher Drainage Failure

1. Clogged Filter Assembly (Most Common)

The Bosch filter system consists of a mesh cylindrical filter and a flat microfilter at the bottom of the tub. Food particles, glass chips, and debris accumulate in both. Bosch recommends monthly cleaning, but most Toronto homeowners go years without cleaning it. A heavily clogged filter is the cause in approximately 40% of drainage calls we receive.

DIY fix: Remove the lower rack, twist and lift the cylindrical filter counterclockwise, remove the flat filter beneath it, and rinse both under running water. Use a soft brush to clear the mesh. Reassemble and run a rinse cycle. If E24 clears, the filter was the issue.

2. Blocked Drain Hose

The drain hose runs from the drain pump to the drain under your kitchen sink. It can become kinked during installation, blocked with grease buildup, or partially blocked where it connects to the sink drain or air gap. A high loop or air gap that is not installed correctly also causes drainage issues in Bosch dishwashers.

Check: Pull the dishwasher slightly forward and inspect the drain hose for sharp bends or kinks. Also check the connection at the sink drain or disposal for blockage.

3. Faulty Drain Pump

The drain pump physically forces water out through the drain hose. When it fails mechanically (worn impeller, seized motor) or electrically (burned-out coil), water remains in the tub. You may hear a humming sound at the end of a cycle if the pump motor is trying to run but cannot expel water.

Diagnosis: After cleaning the filter, run a short cycle and listen at the end. A running pump sound with no drainage indicates a failed pump rather than a clog. This requires professional pump replacement.

4. Garbage Disposal Knockout Plug

If your Bosch was recently installed and has never drained properly, and you have a garbage disposal, the disposal's dishwasher inlet knockout plug may not have been removed during installation. This plug must be knocked out before connecting the dishwasher drain hose. New installations that drain immediately after setup are the giveaway.

5. Control Board or Sensor Fault

Less commonly, the control board misreads a water level sensor and stops the drain cycle early, or the drain pump relay on the board fails. This is the rarest cause and is typically only suspected after the filter, hose, and pump have been confirmed functional.

What You Can Try Before Calling a Technician

  1. Clean the filter (cylindrical + flat mesh) — takes 5 minutes
  2. Check the drain hose for kinks or disconnection at the sink
  3. Run a drain cycle reset: On most Bosch models, hold the Start button for 3 seconds to initiate a drain-only cycle
  4. Check the air gap (if installed) — remove the cap and clear any debris

If E24 persists after these steps, or if you can hear the pump motor running but water is not moving, call for professional service. The pump or hose requires hands-on inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my Bosch dishwasher not draining and showing E24?

E24 means the dishwasher cannot drain. Start by cleaning the filter assembly (cylindrical + flat filter at the bottom of the tub). If E24 clears after cleaning, the filter was the cause. If it persists, the drain pump or hose requires professional inspection.

Can I fix a Bosch E24 error myself?

Yes, if the cause is a clogged filter. Remove and clean the filter, then run a drain cycle. If E24 persists after filter cleaning, the repair requires professional diagnosis and likely drain pump replacement — not a DIY repair for most homeowners.
