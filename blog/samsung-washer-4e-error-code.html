What Does the Samsung 4E Error Code Mean?

Samsung's 4E error (displayed as 4C on newer models with the revised display format) is a water supply fault. The machine's control board monitors how quickly the drum fills during the intake phase. If the drum does not reach the target water level within a set time — typically 7 to 10 minutes depending on the model — the control board triggers 4E and halts the cycle to prevent dry running the pump.

The error appears on both front-load and top-load Samsung washers, across the WF, WA, and Bespoke series. On front-loaders the error typically appears within the first few minutes of a new cycle. On top-loaders it can also appear mid-cycle if the water level sensor detects a drop below the minimum threshold during agitation.

What Are the Most Common Causes of the 4E Error?

Based on service calls across Toronto since 2017, Alex M. ranks the causes in order of frequency:

  • Supply valves partially or fully closed: The hot and cold shut-off valves behind the washer must both be fully open. Even a quarter-turn restriction will reduce flow enough to trigger 4E in some models.
  • Clogged inlet mesh filters: Samsung uses fine mesh screens at the inlet valve connection points. Toronto's water contains minerals that accumulate over time, restricting flow. These screens are frequently overlooked and are the simplest DIY fix.
  • Kinked or pinched inlet hoses: Moving the machine for cleaning or maintenance often kinks the hoses behind the unit.
  • Failed water inlet valve solenoid: The valve itself contains two solenoid-actuated ports (hot and cold). Either solenoid can fail electrically, blocking water from one or both ports entirely.
  • Low household water pressure: Samsung requires a minimum of 30 PSI. Older Toronto homes — particularly pre-1970 construction in areas like Leslieville or The Junction — can have supply pressures below this threshold, especially during morning peak demand hours.
  • Faulty water level pressure sensor: Less common, but a failed pressure sensor can incorrectly report the drum as full or empty, confusing the control board.

Safety warning: Before inspecting the inlet hoses or filters, turn off both water supply valves and unplug the washer from the outlet. Water under pressure will spray forcefully when the hose connection is loosened. Place a towel under the connection before removing hoses.

How Do Technicians Diagnose the 4E Error Precisely?

1

Pressure test at inlet

Alex attaches a calibrated pressure gauge to the cold water inlet port. A reading below 30 PSI at static pressure indicates a building supply issue, not an appliance fault.

2

Filter inspection and flow rate test

Both inlet mesh screens are removed and inspected under LED light. Blocked filters are cleaned or replaced. A bucket fill test measures actual flow rate in litres per minute at the inlet.

3

Inlet valve solenoid resistance test

Each solenoid coil is tested with a multimeter. Samsung cold-water solenoids should read 1,000–1,200 ohms; hot-water solenoids, 800–1,000 ohms. An OL reading indicates a burnt-out solenoid coil.

4

Pressure sensor test

The water level pressure sensor is tested by blowing gently into the sensor hose and measuring the voltage shift at the control board connector with the machine in diagnostic mode (activated by the Fixlify diagnostic tablet).

When Should You Call a Technician vs. DIY?

The inlet filter cleaning is a safe and straightforward DIY task for most homeowners. Everything else — solenoid replacement, pressure sensor swap, or addressing low building water pressure — is best handled by a certified technician. Incorrect reassembly of the inlet valve on a pressurised water connection can result in a significant water leak inside the machine cabinet, leading to floor damage and potential mould issues in Toronto's humid summer months.

Maria S., our senior technician, notes that a common mistake homeowners make is replacing the inlet valve when the real issue is supply pressure. That is a $200 parts spend that does not fix the error. Proper pressure testing at the service call prevents unnecessary part replacement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 4E mean on a Samsung washer?
The 4E (or 4C on newer Samsung displays) error code signals that the washer did not receive enough water within the allotted fill time. It indicates a water supply problem: closed inlet valve, kinked hose, clogged mesh filter, or a faulty water inlet valve solenoid.
Can I fix the Samsung 4E error myself?
Yes, for external causes. Check that both hot and cold shut-off valves behind the washer are fully open, inspect inlet hoses for kinks, and clean the mesh inlet filters at the back of the machine. If the error persists after these steps, the inlet valve solenoid or water pressure sensor likely needs professional replacement.
How much does Samsung 4E error repair cost in Toronto?
Cleaning inlet filters costs $80–$100 (labour only). Replacing the water inlet valve assembly on a Samsung washer typically costs $160–$240 in Toronto including parts and labour. Fixlify provides a digital upfront quote before work begins.
Could low water pressure trigger the 4E error?
Yes. Samsung washers require a minimum of 30 PSI dynamic water pressure to fill correctly. In older Toronto homes with galvanised supply pipes, pressure can drop below this threshold, triggering 4E. A technician can verify supply pressure with a gauge at the inlet valve connection.
