TL;DR: LG refrigerator error codes appear on the display panel and each maps to a specific subsystem fault. The most common codes in Toronto service calls are Er dH (defrost heater), Er FF (freezer fan), Er CO (communication error), and Er rS (refrigerator sensor). Do not just reset and ignore — these indicate real hardware failures that will worsen.

How Do LG Refrigerator Error Codes Work?

LG's inverter linear compressor refrigerators and French door models use a distributed control architecture with a main PCB (main board), a sub-PCB (dispenser/display board), and a series of NTC temperature sensors. When any sensor or subsystem reports a value outside the programmed acceptable range for more than a defined period, the main board logs the fault and displays a two or three character error code on the panel.

Alex M., our lead technician, uses LG SmartDiagnosis — which transmits coded audio tones from the fridge to our diagnostic tablet — to read stored fault history and current sensor values simultaneously. This means we identify not just what is currently failing, but what may have failed intermittently over the past 72 hours, giving a much clearer picture of the real cause.

What Does Er dH Mean on an LG Refrigerator?

Er dH is a defrost heater fault. The refrigerator attempted an automatic defrost cycle but the evaporator temperature sensor (the Defrost Sensor, typically NTC part ADC73959902 or similar) did not report the expected temperature rise during the heater-on period. The control board concludes the defrost heater is not generating heat.

The actual failed component is most often the defrost heater assembly itself — a glass-sheathed resistance wire element coiled around the evaporator — or the defrost thermostat (a thermal cutout that opens if the heater reaches an unsafe temperature). Replacing just one without checking both is a common mistake. On models with a defrost relay on the main board, the relay can also fail in the open position, preventing heater activation.

What Does Er FF Mean — and Is It Dangerous?

Er FF signals a Freezer Fan error. The freezer evaporator fan circulates cold air from the evaporator coils throughout the fresh food and freezer compartments. The fan motor transmits a tachometer (RPM feedback) signal to the main board. If this signal drops below the expected RPM for more than 3 minutes, the board triggers Er FF.

The consequences are significant: without the fan running, the evaporator coils will quickly frost over (which may appear as a concurrent Er dH), and the fresh food compartment will warm to room temperature even though the compressor is still running. Food safety in the fresh food section is compromised within 4 hours. A technician needs to determine whether the fan motor itself has failed electrically, whether the fan blade is physically jammed by ice build-up, or whether the wiring harness has an open circuit.

What Are Er CO, Er rS, Er IS and Other LG Codes?

  • Er CO: Communication error between the main board and sub-board. Usually caused by a loose or corroded connector in the communication harness, or a failed sub-board. Check the harness connection first before replacing boards.
  • Er rS: Refrigerator compartment temperature sensor fault. The NTC sensor in the fresh food section is reading out of range (typically below -50°C or above +70°C equivalent resistance). Sensor replacement is usually $120–$160.
  • Er IS: Ice maker sensor fault. The ice maker compartment temperature sensor has failed or the ice maker assembly itself needs replacement.
  • Er gF: Freezer temperature sensor fault — same logic as Er rS but for the freezer NTC sensor.
  • Er CF: Condenser fan motor error. The condenser fan at the back of the unit, which cools the compressor and condenser coils, is not responding. This error will cause the compressor to overheat and the refrigerator to warm.

Safety warning: If your LG refrigerator is showing any fan or compressor error code, move temperature-sensitive food and medication to a backup fridge or cooler immediately. Compressor temperatures can spike rapidly when fan cooling is lost, potentially tripping thermal overload and damaging the compressor — turning a $180 fan motor repair into a $600+ compressor job.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Er dH mean on an LG refrigerator?
Er dH is a defrost heater error. The control board attempted a defrost cycle but the evaporator temperature sensor did not detect the expected temperature rise, indicating the defrost heater or defrost thermostat has failed. This needs professional repair as evaporator coils will continue to ice over.
What does Er FF mean on an LG fridge?
Er FF stands for Freezer Fan error. The control board is not detecting expected RPM feedback from the freezer evaporator fan motor. The fan motor, fan blade, or wiring harness needs inspection. A blocked fan from ice build-up can also trigger this code.
How much does it cost to repair LG fridge error codes in Toronto?
Repair costs vary by component: defrost heater replacement $160–$220, fan motor replacement $180–$260, temperature sensor $120–$160, main control board $280–$420. Fixlify provides a digital upfront quote based on diagnostic findings before any work is approved.
Can I reset LG fridge error codes myself?
You can clear some LG error codes by unplugging the refrigerator for 60 seconds. However, if the underlying fault is not repaired, the code will return within hours or days. Error codes represent real hardware faults — clearing the code without fixing the cause only delays the repair and risks food spoilage.
