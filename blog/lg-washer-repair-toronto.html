LG Washer Error Codes: OE, LE, tE, UE

LG washers use a clear error code system that points directly to the affected component. In Toronto service calls, the four most frequent LG error codes are OE (drain), LE (motor), tE (temperature sensor) and UE (balance). Identifying the code is the first step to a fast, cost-effective repair.

OE Error — Drain Failure

OE means the washer failed to drain within the expected time. Common causes include a clogged drain filter (the mesh filter behind the access panel at the bottom front), a blocked drain hose, or a failed drain pump motor. LG recommends cleaning the filter every 30 cycles. When the filter is clear and OE persists, the pump impeller is likely damaged or the pump motor winding has burned out. Pump replacement takes 45–60 minutes and parts are available same-day for most WM and WT series models.

LE Error — Motor Overload or Hall Sensor

The LE code indicates a motor problem. LG Direct Drive motor is highly reliable, but the rotor hall sensor that reports motor position can fail, causing a false LE even when the motor itself is fine. A genuine motor failure is rarer but happens on high-mileage machines. Our technicians test the stator resistance and hall sensor output before recommending motor replacement, saving customers hundreds of dollars on unnecessary parts.

tE Error — Thermistor Failure

tE signals a faulty water temperature sensor (thermistor). Without accurate temperature readings, the control board cannot regulate the heating element, so it aborts the cycle. A thermistor replacement is a 20-minute job and typically costs $110–$140 all-in. If the heating element itself has failed, replacement runs $160–$210.

LG Direct Drive Motor — What Makes It Different

Unlike conventional washers with belt-and-pulley drive systems, LG Direct Drive motors attach the rotor directly to the drum shaft, eliminating belts and motor brushes. This means fewer moving parts and longer motor life. Signs of Direct Drive issues include:

  • Loud clunking at start of spin cycle
  • LE code returning after reset
  • Drum turning slowly only in one direction
  • Burning smell during spin

LG TurboWash and Steam Models

LG TurboWash 360 and Steam models add a steam generator and additional water inlets. Steam nozzle blockages can cause water hammer noise and E6 errors. We carry steam generator kits for WM4500H, WM5500H and WM6500H series. If your LG washer uses ThinQ Wi-Fi and the app shows an error, the same fault code applies — bring the code to our technician for a precise diagnosis.

LG Washer Maintenance Tips From Our Toronto Technicians

After servicing hundreds of LG washers across Toronto, North York, Scarborough and Etobicoke, our technicians see the same preventable problems repeatedly. Here's what we recommend to extend your LG washer's lifespan.

Clean the Drain Filter Every 30 Cycles

The drain filter — located behind the small access panel at the bottom front of front-load LG models — collects lint, coins, buttons and debris. A clogged filter is the number-one cause of OE errors and drain pump failures. LG recommends cleaning it every 30 cycles, but most Toronto homeowners have never opened the panel. A five-minute cleaning prevents a $160–$220 pump replacement.

Use HE Detergent in the Right Amount

LG front-load washers require HE (High Efficiency) low-sudsing detergent. Using regular detergent, or too much HE detergent, creates excess suds that don't rinse properly, leaving residue on the drum and door gasket. Over time this causes the rubber seal to retain moisture and develop mould. Use 1–2 tablespoons of liquid HE detergent per normal load — significantly less than the measuring cup lines suggest.

Run Tub Clean Monthly

LG's Tub Clean cycle (select Tub Clean, run empty) uses high-temperature water to flush biofilm, detergent residue and mineral deposits from the drum and internal plumbing. In Toronto's hard water conditions (around 120–180 mg/L calcium carbonate), mineral buildup accelerates. Running Tub Clean monthly with an LG-approved drum cleaner prevents the musty odour complaints we hear frequently from Toronto customers.

Leave the Door Ajar Between Cycles

Front-load LG washers seal tightly, trapping humidity inside after a wash. Leaving the door open 10–15 cm between loads allows the drum and door gasket to air-dry, significantly reducing mould and mildew growth. Wipe the gasket folds with a dry cloth after laundry day — moisture trapped in the accordion folds is exactly where mould starts. These four habits add years to your machine's service life.
