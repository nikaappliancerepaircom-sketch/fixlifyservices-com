Samsung Washer Error Codes Explained

Samsung front-load and top-load washers communicate problems through error codes on the display. Understanding what each code means helps you decide whether a quick DIY fix is possible or a certified technician is needed. In our Toronto service calls, the four most common Samsung washer error codes are DC, UE, ND (or 5E), and 3E — each pointing to a different system.

DC Error Code — Drum Not Spinning

The DC code means the drum detected an unbalanced load and cannot spin safely. Sometimes redistributing laundry clears it. If the DC code returns consistently, the suspension rods may be worn, the shock absorbers cracked, or the hall sensor that tracks drum position has failed. A worn motor coupling on older models can also trigger DC. Our technicians carry Samsung OEM suspension rods and sensors for same-day repair on most GTS, WF and WA series models.

UE Code — Unbalanced Load or Faulty Sensor

UE (Unbalanced Error) often appears mid-cycle when bulky items clump to one side. However, a persistent UE code that reappears on normal loads points to a failing tachometer sensor, worn drum bearings, or loose spider arm bolts. Left unrepaired, an unbalanced drum wears the shaft seal and can crack the tub. We diagnose UE root cause within 30 minutes on-site.

Drum Bearing Noise — Grinding or Rumbling Sound

A deep grinding or rumbling noise during the spin cycle almost always means drum bearings are failing. Samsung front-loaders use sealed rear bearings pressed into the outer tub. As the seal degrades, water and detergent enter the bearing causing rust and metal-on-metal contact. Early warning signs include:

  • Low hum during slow spin that intensifies at high RPM
  • Slight wobble visible when drum is pushed by hand
  • Rust-coloured water staining inside the drum
  • Squealing on startup before drum warms up

Bearing replacement on a Samsung front-loader requires full tub disassembly. Our Toronto team completes this repair in 2–3 hours on-site and backs the parts with a 90-day warranty.

Control Board and Door Latch Failures

When a Samsung washer shows no display, random errors, or refuses to start, suspect the main control board (PCB) or the door latch assembly. The PCB manages every cycle parameter; a failed relay or blown capacitor disrupts power delivery to the motor and drum heater. Door latch failures prevent the washer from detecting a closed door, so it will not initiate a cycle. Both parts are stocked for WF45, WF50, WA50 and WA54 series.

Washer Maintenance Tips That Prevent Costly Repairs

After years of servicing washers in Toronto and surrounding areas, our technicians see the same preventable failures time after time. Here are the habits that keep repair bills away.

Clean the Drain Filter Monthly

Front-load washers have a drain filter behind the small access panel at the bottom front. This filter catches lint, coins, buttons and small debris before they reach the pump. A clogged filter is the leading cause of drain errors and pump failures. Cleaning it takes three minutes and costs nothing — ignoring it can lead to a $150–$220 pump replacement. On top-load models, run a cleaning cycle with a washing machine tablet monthly.

Always Use HE Detergent

High-efficiency washers require HE-labelled, low-sudsing detergent. Regular detergent creates excessive suds that the machine cannot rinse away properly, leading to residue buildup on the drum, hoses and door gasket. Over time this residue harbours bacteria, causes musty odours and accelerates gasket deterioration. Use liquid HE detergent in the amount printed on the cap — typically far less than you'd expect.

Check the Hoses Annually

Washer inlet hoses (hot and cold water supply) are the leading cause of household flooding. Rubber hoses develop micro-cracks over time and can fail catastrophically. Inspect both hoses annually for bulging, cracking or corrosion at the fittings. Braided stainless steel hoses are a worthwhile upgrade — they rarely fail and cost about $25 at any hardware store. Replace rubber hoses every 5 years regardless of appearance.

Following these three steps can easily extend your washer's service life by several years. When problems do arise, call us for same-day diagnosis in Toronto.
