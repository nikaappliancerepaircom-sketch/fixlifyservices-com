Problem 1: Dishes Not Getting Clean

Clogged Spray Arms

KitchenAid dishwashers use upper and lower spray arms with small holes that can clog with lime scale and food debris. When clogged, water pressure drops and dishes in certain zones don't get cleaned. DIY fix: remove the spray arms (they twist off), rinse them under hot water, and poke a toothpick through any blocked holes. If cleaning doesn't restore pressure, the wash pump may need replacement.

Failed Wash Pump or Motor

The wash pump forces water through the spray arms at high pressure. When the motor bearings wear out or the impeller cracks, water pressure drops significantly — dishes look like they were just rinsed, not washed. Signs: a hissing or grinding noise during the wash cycle, or noticeably poor cleaning performance. Wash pump replacement: $200–$340.

Water Temperature Too Low

KitchenAid recommends inlet water temperature of at least 120°F (49°C) for proper cleaning. If your Toronto home's hot water heater is set too low, or if the dishwasher's heating element has failed, water temperature won't be sufficient to activate detergent enzymes and remove grease. Test: run the kitchen faucet until it's hot before starting the dishwasher. If cleaning improves, check your water heater setpoint. If not, the dishwasher's heating element may have failed (error code 9-1).

Problem 2: Not Draining

Clogged Filter Assembly (DIY Fix)

KitchenAid dishwashers have a two-part filter system at the tub floor — a coarse screen and a fine cylindrical filter. These must be cleaned monthly. Remove the lower rack, twist and lift the cylindrical filter, remove the flat screen beneath it, and rinse both under hot water with a brush. Reinstall and run a rinse cycle. This resolves drain problems in many cases at no cost.

Failed Drain Pump

If the filter is clean but the dishwasher still won't drain (water sitting in the bottom after the cycle), the drain pump motor has likely failed. You may hear humming or silence where a drain noise should be. Drain pump replacement: $180–$300. On KitchenAid models, the drain pump is accessed from the front after removing the door panel — a job for a technician.

Problem 3: Not Starting or Mid-Cycle Stops

Door Latch Failure

KitchenAid dishwashers require a fully engaged door latch to run. A worn latch doesn't send the start signal to the control board — the dishwasher appears unresponsive. This is especially common on KitchenAid models with the third rack (KDTE series) where the door is slightly heavier. Door latch replacement: $130–$200.

Control Board Failure

The control board governs every dishwasher function. When it fails, the dishwasher may not respond to button presses, stop mid-cycle randomly, or display persistent error codes. Control board replacement: $250–$420. For KitchenAid models under 5 years old, it's worth checking if the board is covered under extended warranty — contact KitchenAid Canada.
