Gas Range Repair in Edmonton

Surface Burner Won’t Ignite

Surface burner ignition failure is the most common gas range complaint in Edmonton. Modern sealed-burner gas ranges use electronic ignition — a spark igniter that fires when you turn the burner knob. Common causes:

  • Food debris in the igniter port: Boil-overs that seep into the burner cap and igniter well prevent sparking. Remove the burner grate and cap, clean the igniter tip with a dry toothbrush, and ensure the burner cap is seated correctly. This is a DIY fix that resolves about 40% of ignition complaints without any parts.
  • Failed igniter module: Each surface burner has an individual spark igniter. A cracked or burned igniter body produces no spark and needs replacement. Individual igniter replacement: $80–$130.
  • Failed ignition switch module: The switch that triggers sparking when the knob is turned can fail electrically. All burners may stop sparking simultaneously, or one burner's spark may stay on continuously (clicking when not in use). Switch module: $100–$170.

Gas Burner Won’t Light or Has Weak Flame

If the igniter sparks but gas does not flow, the gas valve or burner orifice is restricting flow. Orifices can become partially blocked by debris in the gas supply line (more common after work on the home's gas system or after a new ATCO metre installation). Orifice cleaning or replacement is a technical procedure that requires the burner assembly to be disassembled. Cost: $90–$150. A gas valve solenoid fault can also prevent individual burners from opening — this is a $120–$200 repair.

Gas Oven Not Heating or Heating Unevenly

Gas oven heating failures in Edmonton most commonly involve the oven igniter. Unlike surface burners with open flames, gas ovens use a glow-bar igniter that must reach a specific resistance threshold before the gas valve opens. A weakening (but not yet failed) igniter allows the oven to heat but takes longer than normal, or causes the burner to cycle off before reaching setpoint temperature. A fully failed igniter produces no heat at all. Oven igniter replacement: $100–$160. The oven temperature sensor (thermistor) is a separate component that calibrates temperature — failure causes inaccurate baking. Oven thermistor: $80–$130.

Gas Dryer Repair in Edmonton

Gas dryers are extremely common in Edmonton detached homes, offering faster drying times and lower operating cost than electric equivalents at Alberta's natural gas pricing. When a gas dryer fails to heat, the repair path differs entirely from an electric dryer diagnosis.

Gas Dryer Igniter Failure

The igniter in a gas dryer glows to ignite the gas burner at the start of each heat cycle. A failed igniter produces a dryer that tumbles normally but never heats. Igniter replacement is a 45-minute repair with the dryer pulled away from the wall. Cost: $90–$140.

Gas Valve Coil Kit

Whirlpool-platform gas dryers (covering Whirlpool, Maytag, Kenmore, and Amana brands — very common in Edmonton) develop gas valve coil failures after 6–10 years. The symptom is distinctive: the igniter glows and the burner ignites briefly, then the flame goes out and the cycle repeats without sustaining heat. Gas valve coil kit replacement (typically 2–3 coils sold as a set): $100–$160.

Radiant Sensor (Flame Sensor)

The radiant sensor monitors burner flame presence. A failed sensor can prevent the gas valve from ever opening (no heat) or keep the igniter energized after the flame establishes (wasted energy and potential safety issue). Radiant sensor replacement: $70–$110.

Safety: What Gas Appliance Work Requires a Certified Tech

In Alberta, work on gas appliance components that could affect gas flow — gas valves, pressure regulators, gas lines, fittings, and connections — must be performed by a certified gas fitter or a technician working under one. Cosmetic and electrical repairs (igniter replacement, control board, oven thermistor) that do not involve disturbing gas connections can be performed by trained appliance technicians.
