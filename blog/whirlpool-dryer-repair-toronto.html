Why Whirlpool Dryers Stop Heating

A Whirlpool dryer that runs but produces no heat is one of the most common appliance complaints in Toronto. The heating circuit passes through several components in series — if any single part fails, the entire heating function cuts out. The three most common culprits in order of frequency are the thermal fuse, the heating element coil, and the high-limit thermostat.

Thermal Fuse — Most Common No-Heat Cause

The thermal fuse is a one-shot safety device mounted on the exhaust duct inside the dryer. If the exhaust temperature exceeds a safe threshold (usually due to a blocked lint duct or failed cycling thermostat), the fuse blows and permanently breaks the heating circuit. It cannot be reset — it must be replaced. A thermal fuse replacement costs $80–$120 all-in. Critically, the root cause (duct blockage, cycling thermostat failure) must be fixed at the same time, or the new fuse will blow again within weeks.

Heating Element — Visible Break in the Coil

Electric Whirlpool dryers use a coiled resistance wire mounted inside a metal housing. Over 8–12 years, thermal cycling causes the coil to develop a break. A multimeter test confirms the fault in minutes. Heating element replacement on standard Whirlpool cabinets takes about 60 minutes. Parts run $70–$100; total repair is typically $180–$240.

Drum Seal — The Source of Thumping and Heat Loss

Whirlpool dryer drums ride on a felt seal that prevents hot air from escaping around the drum perimeter. When the seal wears through, you hear a rhythmic thumping as the drum rubs on the cabinet. Heat also escapes from the drum cavity, making drying times 50–70% longer. A front drum seal kit for common Whirlpool models (WED, MED series) is $40–$60 in parts; labour brings the total to $160–$210.

Whirlpool Dryer Belt and Idler Pulley

If the motor runs but the drum does not turn, the drive belt has broken. Whirlpool uses a long poly-v belt routed around the drum, an idler pulley, and the motor pulley. Replacing just the belt often makes sense only if the idler pulley and drum bearing are still smooth — otherwise expect the job back within a year. We recommend replacing belt, idler pulley, and drum support rollers as a set for maximum longevity. This package runs $200–$270.

Gas vs Electric Whirlpool Dryer Repairs

Gas Whirlpool dryers use an igniter and gas valve coils instead of a resistance heating element. The igniter glows red-hot to ignite the gas flame; if it cracks (a common failure after 5–8 years), the burner never lights. Gas valve coils fail independently, causing intermittent heating. What to Expect During a Whirlpool Dryer Repair in Toronto

Dryer repairs begin with a safety check — we test the thermal fuse (the most commonly replaced dryer component) and check that the exhaust temperature is within safe range. An overheating dryer is a fire risk, so our technicians always measure exhaust heat before running a full diagnostic cycle.

Dryer Maintenance Tips: Reduce Fire Risk and Extend Appliance Life

Dryers cause approximately 15,000 house fires per year in North America, nearly all from lint buildup. These tips protect your home and extend your dryer's service life significantly.

Clean the Lint Trap After Every Load

This is the most important habit. A clogged lint trap reduces airflow, forces the heating element to work harder, extends drying times and creates a fire hazard. Beyond the trap itself, lint accumulates in the duct system over time. Wash the lint screen with soap and water monthly to remove the invisible film left by dryer sheets, which restricts airflow even when the trap looks clean.

Vent Cleaning Annually (or Every Six Months for Heavy Use)

The dryer exhaust duct collects lint that bypasses the trap. A blocked duct is the primary cause of dryer fires and dramatically reduces efficiency. Signs of a blocked duct: clothes take two cycles to dry, the dryer exterior is unusually hot, or you smell burning during operation. Professional vent cleaning costs $80–$120 in Toronto and should be done annually. Use only rigid or semi-rigid metal duct — flexible plastic duct crushes easily and collects lint at every bend.

Keep the Area Around the Dryer Clear

Combustible materials (clothing, lint-covered surfaces, cleaning supplies) should not be stored near dryers. Ensure at least 30 cm of clearance on all sides for proper ventilation. Gas dryers require an approved gas line installation — never use flexible connectors longer than 1.8 metres or run them through walls. If you smell gas at any point, leave the home and call your gas utility immediately.
