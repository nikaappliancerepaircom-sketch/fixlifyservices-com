What Is the Samsung UE Error Code and When Does It Appear?

Samsung displays the UE error (labelled Ub on older pre-2013 control panel models) when the vibration sensor detects excessive drum imbalance during the acceleration to high-speed spin. The machine attempts to rebalance the load automatically — adding a small amount of water, then slowly spinning the drum to redistribute laundry — up to three times before displaying UE and pausing the cycle.

The vibration sensor used on Samsung washers is a piezoelectric accelerometer mounted to the tub. It reads vibration in three axes and reports to the main control board. On current VRT Plus (Vibration Reduction Technology) models, the sensor sampling rate is high enough to detect not just the amplitude of vibration but its frequency pattern, which allows the control board to distinguish between a simple load imbalance and a mechanical issue like a worn damper.

Is the UE Error Always a Load Problem?

No. Load imbalance is the most common trigger and is easy to fix yourself. But in a significant percentage of service calls, the UE error is a symptom of mechanical wear that is making it impossible for the machine to tolerate even a moderately uneven load distribution. Signs that the problem is mechanical rather than load-related:

  • UE appears on small, light loads that should never cause imbalance
  • Loud banging or thumping during the spin cycle even before UE appears
  • The machine walks across the floor during spin despite being properly levelled
  • UE appears on every spin cycle regardless of load size or composition
  • The drum feels loose when you push it side-to-side with the door open

What Mechanical Parts Cause Persistent UE Errors?

Alex M. identifies four mechanical failure points in order of frequency for Samsung front-loaders in Toronto:

  • Shock absorbers (dampers): Two or four hydraulic damper struts connect the drum tub to the cabinet frame. They absorb vibration during spin. After 5–8 years of use, the internal oil film is depleted and the damper becomes loose and bouncy instead of controlled. This is the most common mechanical cause of UE errors on Samsung washers 5+ years old.
  • Suspension springs: Three or four tension springs at the top of the tub support it in the cabinet. A broken or over-stretched spring allows the drum to hang lower on one side, creating a permanent imbalance that the machine cannot compensate for.
  • Front counterbalance weight: A cast concrete or plastic weight bolted to the front of the tub counteracts drum rotation forces. If the mounting bolts loosen, the weight shifts and the entire mass balance of the tub changes.
  • Drum bearings: Failed rear drum bearings create lateral play in the drum — the drum can shift side-to-side during high-speed spin, triggering UE. This is the most expensive repair and produces a loud grinding noise well before the bearing fails completely.

Safety warning: Do not continue running a washer that is banging severely during spin. If the UE error appears alongside loud impacts, the machine may be experiencing drum strike against the cabinet walls — which can damage the drum, the door seal, and in severe cases crack the tub. Pause the cycle and contact a technician before the next use.

How Does Fixlify Diagnose the Samsung UE Error?

1

Level check and load test

Machine level is verified with a digital level tool — all four feet must contact the floor with less than 2mm deviation. A controlled test load (3kg of mixed items) is run. If UE still appears, mechanical inspection begins.

2

Drum play check

Front panel removed. Technician manually moves the drum tub horizontally and vertically. Excessive play in any direction indicates worn dampers, broken springs, or failed bearings.

3

Damper compression test

Each shock absorber is compressed by hand and released. A functional damper resists compression and returns slowly. A worn damper compresses with little resistance and springs back quickly — confirming failure.

4

Component replacement and spin verification

Failed components replaced. Machine run through a high-spin test cycle (1200+ RPM) with a measured test load. Vibration level is confirmed acceptable before reassembly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does UE mean on a Samsung washer?
UE (or Ub on some older Samsung models) means the drum is unbalanced during the spin cycle. The machine's vibration sensor detected excessive imbalance and stopped spinning to prevent damage. This is either a load distribution problem (easy DIY fix) or a mechanical fault such as worn shock absorbers, a failed suspension spring, or a faulty drum balance ring.
How do I fix the Samsung UE error myself?
Open the door and redistribute the laundry evenly around the drum. Avoid washing single heavy items like a duvet alone — add a few smaller items to balance the load. Ensure the machine is level; adjust the levelling feet if needed. If UE continues on properly loaded, balanced cycles, a mechanical inspection is needed.
What mechanical parts cause persistent Samsung UE errors?
Worn shock absorbers (dampers) are the most common mechanical cause — they are consumed by normal use and lose their damping ability after 5–8 years. Broken or stretched suspension springs, a cracked counterbalance weight, and failed drum bearings (which cause lateral play) can all generate persistent UE errors.
