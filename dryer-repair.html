Professional Dryer Repair Across Toronto & the GTA

When your dryer stops heating, refuses to tumble, or takes three cycles to dry a single load, laundry backs up fast. Fixlify Appliance Services provides same-day dryer repair to homes across Toronto and the GTA, getting your machine back to full performance without unnecessary delays or inflated pricing.

Dryers come in two main varieties — electric and gas — and each has distinct failure patterns. Electric dryers rely on a resistive heating element, a thermal fuse, a cycling thermostat, and a high-limit thermostat to regulate heat. When any of these components fails, the dryer runs but produces no heat, which is the single most common dryer complaint we handle across the GTA. Gas dryers use an igniter and gas valve coils to produce heat, and these components wear out over time, especially in older homes where the dryer runs daily for larger families.

Beyond heating issues, dryers can develop problems with the drum belt, drum rollers, idler pulley, blower wheel, or the motor itself. A squealing or thumping noise during operation usually points to worn drum rollers or a frayed belt. Clothes taking unusually long to dry can signal a clogged vent line — a safety issue that also drives up your electricity or gas bill. Our technicians inspect the entire system on every service call to catch secondary problems before they become expensive failures.

Dryer Vent Safety

A clogged dryer vent is more than an inconvenience — it is a fire hazard. Lint accumulates in the exhaust duct over time, restricting airflow and causing the dryer to overheat. The Canadian Fire Commissioner reports that dryer lint buildup is a leading cause of residential fires. We recommend professional vent cleaning every 12-18 months, and our technicians inspect the vent condition during every repair visit at no additional charge.

How We Diagnose Dryer Problems in Toronto & GTA

When a Fixlify technician arrives at your Toronto & GTA home, the diagnostic process is methodical and thorough. We do not guess — we test. Toronto & GTA presents its own challenges: wide variation in water hardness (120-200+ mg/L), housing age (pre-war to new-build), and appliance brands across the region. Our technicians are trained specifically for the conditions found in Toronto & GTA homes, from condos, townhomes, detached houses, and multi-unit properties spanning from downtown Toronto to the outer suburbs. Here is exactly how we isolate the fault:

  1. Lint & Airflow Assessment: Restricted airflow is the root cause of most dryer failures. We start by inspecting the lint trap, the internal lint chute, and the external vent duct from the dryer to the outside wall. A partially blocked vent forces the dryer to overheat, tripping the thermal fuse or burning out the heating element prematurely.
  2. Thermal System Check: We test the heating element (electric) or igniter and gas valve coils (gas) along with the high-limit thermostat, cycling thermostat, and thermal fuse. These components work as a safety chain — if one fails, the dryer either will not heat at all or will overheat dangerously. We test each one individually with a multimeter.
  3. Mechanical & Motor Inspection: Drum rollers, idler pulleys, and drive belts wear out over time. A squeaking, thumping, or rattling dryer almost always has a worn drum roller or a fraying belt. We check belt tension, roller bearing play, and motor amperage draw to isolate the noise source precisely.
  4. Control & Sensor Verification: Modern dryers use moisture sensors and electronic control boards to regulate cycle length and temperature. We test sensor bar continuity, board relay outputs, and door switch function. We explain every finding before recommending repair — you always know the cost before we start.

Maintenance Tips for Dryer Owners in Toronto & GTA

Preventive maintenance is the most cost-effective way to extend your dryer's lifespan and avoid emergency repair calls. These five tips are specifically relevant for Toronto & GTA homes, where wide variation in water hardness (120-200+ mg/l) plays a significant role in appliance wear:

  1. Clean the lint trap before every single load — even a thin layer of lint reduces airflow and increases drying time
  2. Have the full vent duct professionally cleaned once a year, especially if your duct run is longer than 10 feet or has multiple elbows
  3. Never overload the dryer — an overstuffed drum restricts tumbling and forces the heating element to work harder
  4. Wipe down the moisture sensor bars inside the drum every month with rubbing alcohol to keep auto-dry cycles accurate
  5. Check the exterior vent flap quarterly — birds and wasps frequently nest inside dryer vent hoods in the GTA

Following these steps can extend your dryer's life by 3-5 years and reduce the likelihood of an unexpected breakdown. If you notice anything unusual despite regular maintenance, book a diagnostic visit through Fixlify — catching a small problem early almost always costs less than waiting until it fails completely.
