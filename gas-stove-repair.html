Gas Stove and Gas Range Repair — TSSA Licensed, All Brands

The gas stove is the one kitchen appliance where performance genuinely depends on the fuel source. Professional chefs and serious home cooks choose gas because the flame responds instantly to control adjustments — there is no coil warm-up delay, no induction compatibility requirement, and no radiant surface that retains heat after the knob is turned off. That responsiveness comes from an open flame burning natural gas in your kitchen, which is why gas stove repair is regulated more strictly than any other household appliance service in Ontario. Every gas stove repair by Fixlify Appliance Services is performed by a TSSA-licensed gas fitter who carries the training, certification, and diagnostic equipment the job demands.

Gas Stove Emergency Response Steps

Gas stoves produce open flames in your Toronto kitchen. Enbridge distributes natural gas to over 500,000 Toronto households. When a gas stove malfunctions, the response protocol differs from electric appliance failures.

  1. Turn all burner knobs on your Toronto gas stove to the OFF position immediately
  2. Do not attempt to relight a burner that has gone out — unburned gas may have pooled above the cooktop
  3. Open kitchen windows and the nearest exterior door for cross-ventilation
  4. If gas odour persists with all controls off, evacuate and dial 911 from outside
  5. Call Enbridge Gas Emergency at 1-866-763-5427 for professional leak investigation

Under Ontario's Technical Standards and Safety Act, only TSSA-certified gas fitters may service gas stoves. Toronto Fire Services responds to approximately 300 gas-related calls annually across the city.

Gas Stove Components That Fail — And What Each Repair Costs

Gas stoves have two distinct sections — the cooktop burners and the oven — each with its own ignition system, gas supply, and failure patterns:

Cooktop Burner Issues

  • Spark igniter failure: The ceramic electrode produces a spark to light the gas stream. Fails from thermal cracking, grease contamination, or spark module malfunction. Repair: $80 to $150.
  • Burner port clogging: Food residue, grease, and mineral deposits block the small gas ports around the burner cap, producing an uneven flame, yellow tips, or partial lighting. Cleaning: $60 to $100. Burner cap replacement: $80 to $140.
  • Gas valve leak at burner stem: Turning the knob should produce a smooth, proportional gas flow. A worn valve stem seal allows gas to seep even in the off position. Valve replacement: $150 to $250 per burner.

Oven Section Issues

  • Igniter glow bar degradation: The most common gas stove oven repair across the entire GTA. The silicon carbide glow bar must draw sufficient amperage to open the gas safety valve. Repair: $150 to $250.
  • Gas safety valve failure: If the valve body develops an internal seal failure, the oven will not heat regardless of igniter condition. This is the less common cause of oven no-heat but costs more to repair: $200 to $350.
  • Electronic control board: Modern gas stoves with digital displays, timer functions, and electronic oven controls rely on a main control board. Board failure can cause erratic burner behavior, temperature inaccuracy, or complete shutdown. Repair: $200 to $400.

How does Fixlify Appliance Services diagnose a gas stove oven that does not heat?+

We start with igniter amperage testing using a clamp meter on the igniter lead wire. A healthy igniter draws 3.2 to 3.6 amps. Below 3.0 amps, the igniter cannot open the gas safety valve even though it may appear to glow normally. This single test identifies the root cause in over 80 percent of gas stove oven no-heat complaints without unnecessary gas valve replacement.
