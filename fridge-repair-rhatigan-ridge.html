Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Rhatigan Ridge

Frequent refrigerator faults in Rhatigan Ridge's premium 1980s–90s homes and modernized kitchens.

KA Ice Maker

KitchenAid Ice Maker Failure

KitchenAid built-in refrigerators in Rhatigan Ridge develop ice maker failures from inlet valve calcium deposits — common with Edmonton's hard EPCOR water (170–200 mg/L) — or from ice maker module wear. Ice production slows or stops entirely. We carry KitchenAid-compatible ice maker assemblies and flush the water line as part of repair.

Samsung FF

Samsung Four-Door Flex Fan Fault

Samsung four-door Flex and French-door models in renovated Rhatigan Ridge kitchens display FF or error codes from evaporator fan motor seizure in the freezer or flex-zone compartments. We carry Samsung-specific dual-evaporator fan motors and ice maker assemblies for same-visit resolution.

Thermistor

Temperature Sensor (Thermistor) Failure

KitchenAid and GE Profile refrigerators use multiple thermistors to regulate temperature independently in different zones. A failed thermistor causes one zone to over-cool or warm unpredictably, often while the display reads normal. We test all thermistors during diagnostics and replace failed sensors with OEM parts.

Dual Evap

Dual Evaporator Defrost Failure

KitchenAid built-in fridges with dual-evaporator systems develop independent defrost failures in each zone. Frost accumulates on one evaporator while the other functions normally, causing temperature imbalances. Diagnosing dual-evap systems requires KitchenAid-specific knowledge — our technicians are trained on these platforms.

Water Inlet

Water Inlet Valve & Dispenser Failure

Hard water mineral deposits from EPCOR's supply (170–200 mg/L) cause water inlet valves in KitchenAid and Samsung models to fail gradually — dispenser flow slows, then ice production drops, then stops. We replace the inlet valve, install a pre-filter recommendation, and flush the water system during repair.

Control Board

Control Board & Display Panel Failure

Premium KitchenAid and GE Profile refrigerators use advanced control boards that fail due to power surge damage or age-related capacitor failure. Symptoms include unresponsive controls, erratic temperature behavior, or blank display panels. We diagnose with OEM diagnostic tools and replace boards with manufacturer-compatible parts.
