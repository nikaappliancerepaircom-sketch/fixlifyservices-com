Samsung Dishwasher Error Codes — What Toronto Technicians See

The 5E drain error is the most common Samsung dishwasher call in Toronto. Samsung's micro-filter system — a fine mesh filter beneath the lower spray arm — traps food particles effectively but clogs faster than competing designs, especially in Toronto households that pre-rinse less than the manual recommends. On many 5E calls, cleaning the micro-filter resolves the code immediately — a 15-minute service call. When the filter is clear but the 5E persists, the drain pump impeller is usually jammed with a small piece of broken glass or plastic, which we clear on-site. Actual drain pump motor replacements are needed in only about 25% of 5E calls.

The 7E WaterWall reflector error is specific to Samsung's DW80K "WaterWall" series — a distinctive model that uses a sliding reflector bar instead of traditional rotating spray arms. The reflector motor is a brushless DC motor that develops bearing wear after 3–5 years. Early symptoms include grinding noise during wash cycles before the 7E code appears. Fixlify stocks WaterWall reflector motors for the DW80K7050 and DW80K7054 models, which are the most common Toronto variants. The reflector assembly replaces in about 45 minutes.

Samsung's LC leak-detection system on the DW80R and DW80CG series uses a moisture sensor pad in the base tray — a more reliable design than a mechanical float. When LC appears, we use Samsung's diagnostic mode (hold the Start button for 3 seconds) to distinguish between an active leak and a sensor residue false positive. In about 30% of LC calls in Toronto, the sensor has moisture on it from a previous episode that has since stopped leaking, and drying the base tray resolves the code. When the leak is active, the most common source is the door bottom seal — a $25 part that replaces in 20 minutes.
