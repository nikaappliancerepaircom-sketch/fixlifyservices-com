LG Dishwasher Error Codes — Toronto Service Insights

The AE leak error on LG QuadWash dishwashers in Toronto condos is our most frequent LG call. The flood-protection float in the base tray triggers AE when it detects standing water, but the source is rarely obvious without disassembly. In LDF series models, the most common leak source is the circulation pump inlet seal — a $20 OEM O-ring that deteriorates after 3–5 years of use. A secondary cause is the door gasket, which develops micro-tears in LG's thinner door profiles after heavy use. Our technicians inspect both on every AE call and carry both parts on the van.

The LE motor-locked error is unique to LG's inverter direct-drive dishwashers and typically appears in one of two scenarios: a hard object (usually a piece of broken glass or a plastic label) has lodged in the wash impeller, or the motor winding has developed a ground fault from moisture ingress. We check the impeller manually before testing the motor electrically. In about 60% of LE calls we clear a physical obstruction and the motor tests fine — no parts cost, just labour. In the remaining cases, an LG direct-drive motor replacement ($100–$140 in parts) restores full function.

TrueSteam failures in Toronto are disproportionately common because Toronto's hard water (120–145 mg/L calcium) scales the steam generator nozzle in as little as 18–24 months. LG does not include a descale reminder in older TrueSteam firmware, so many Toronto owners run through the warranty period without descaling and find the steam function dead by year three. Fixlify descales TrueSteam generators on-site in most cases — if the generator element is intact and the nozzle unblocked, the function restores. Annual citric acid rinses prevent recurrence.
