Common issues

Common Washer Problems We Fix in Fort Saskatchewan

These are the washer faults we diagnose most often across Fort Saskatchewan — with Industrial Heartland adjacency driving accelerated wear on filters, condensers, and bearings. Each is a same-day fix in roughly 78% of cases.

LG / Samsung error codes (UE / OE / LE / 1E)
UE = unbalanced load (re-distribute). OE = drain fault. LE = motor overload. 1E = water level sensor. We carry the diagnostic sheets. Most calls in Westpark and Sherridon homes.
Vibration / walking across floor
Suspension rod failure (top-load) or shock absorber wear (front-load). Rods are $30 each + 90 minutes labour. Common pattern in the Southfort developments.
Won't fill / takes forever to fill
Inlet valve solenoid failure or screen clog from hard water. Valves are $60–$90; screens we just clean. Routine fix in Forest Ridge and Pineview.
Won't drain / standing water
Drain pump impeller jam (coins, hair, lint) or pressure switch fault. We pull the pump, clear the impeller, and replace if motor is damaged. Frequent in Ross Hill and Heritage Park.
Won't spin / clothes still wet
Lid lock micro-switch failure (top-load) or door switch interlock (front-load). On front-loaders, also check drive belt and motor coupler. Pattern we see across Industrial Heartland-adjacent homes.
Leaking from underneath
Tub seal failure, hose clamp slipping, or pump body crack. Tub seal replacement is the deepest job — 3 hours on most front-loaders. Most calls in Westpark and Sherridon homes.
Loud thumping / drum bearing roar
Rear drum bearing wear is the #1 long-term failure on front-loaders past 7 years. Bearing kit + tub disassembly; we lay out repair-vs-replace economics. Common pattern in the Southfort developments.
Won't start / no power
Lid lock circuit, door switch, or main control board. Lid lock is the most common — micro-switch defect on Whirlpool WTW-series 2018+ units. Routine fix in Forest Ridge and Pineview.

What does a UE / OE / LE / 1E error code mean on my washer? +

These are LG and Samsung standard codes. UE = unbalanced load (re-distribute the laundry, run again). OE = drain fault (check the drain hose, pump filter, and standpipe). LE = motor overload or rotor lock (often a stuck object or seized bearing). 1E or 4E = water level or inlet valve sensor fault. We carry the diagnostic flowcharts for LG and Samsung and have repaired hundreds of these errors across Fort Saskatchewan.
