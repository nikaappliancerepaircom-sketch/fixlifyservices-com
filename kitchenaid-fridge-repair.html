KitchenAid Refrigerator Repair — Common Issues We Fix

Our technicians have extensive experience with KitchenAid refrigerator repair faults. We diagnose accurately and repair on the first visit in most cases.

Refrigerator Ice Maker Not Working

KitchenAid French Door refrigerators share ice maker assemblies with Whirlpool and develop the same inlet valve and fill tube freeze-up issues. We carry KitchenAid-compatible ice maker kits on our service vehicles.

Refrigerator Fan Noise

KitchenAid French Door refrigerators develop fan noise from worn evaporator or condenser fan motor bearings. We identify the noisy fan and replace the motor assembly before it causes a cooling failure.

KitchenAid Refrigerator Repair FAQ

Do you repair KitchenAid dishwashers — the quietest in North America?
Yes. KitchenAid dishwashers including the ultra-quiet KDFE and KDTE series are fully serviced. Drainage faults, control board errors, and door latch failures are the most common calls we handle.
My KitchenAid oven shows F9 and is locked. How urgent is this?
The F9 error with a locked door should be addressed promptly. The oven is locked for safety but the door lock motor has failed. We can release the lock and repair the motor assembly, typically same-day. Call (437) 524-1053 immediately.
Is KitchenAid worth repairing compared to buying new?
Yes. KitchenAid appliances are priced at $1,000 to $3,000 new. Most repairs cost $150 to $400. Given the quality build and premium price point, repair is almost always the right economic choice unless the appliance is over 15 years old.
Do you carry KitchenAid parts on your service vans?
Yes. KitchenAid shares much of the Whirlpool parts ecosystem, and we stock the most commonly needed components including door latches, ice maker assemblies, door lock motors, and igniter switches on every service vehicle.
