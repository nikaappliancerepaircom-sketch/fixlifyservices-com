GE Dishwasher Repair — Common Issues We Fix

Our technicians have extensive experience with GE dishwasher repair faults. We diagnose accurately and repair on the first visit in most cases.

Refrigerator Not Cooling

GE refrigerators frequently develop evaporator fan motor failures, sealed system leaks, and dirty condenser coil issues. Older GE models in rental homes are particularly susceptible to these faults after years of use.

Washer F7 / E1 Error (Motor)

GE front-load washer motor tachometer and control board faults produce F7 or E1 codes. These indicate the motor is not receiving proper speed feedback. We test the tachometer, wiring harness, and control board to pinpoint the failure.

Oven Igniter Failure (Gas)

GE gas ranges develop igniter failures after years of use. A weak igniter that glows but does not light the burner needs replacement. We carry GE igniter assemblies for both oven igniters and surface burner igniters.

Dishwasher Not Starting

GE dishwashers fail to start from a faulty door latch, broken door switch, or failed control board. GE Profile and GDF series door latch failures are common after several years of use.

GE Dishwasher Repair FAQ

Do you repair GE Profile and Cafe appliances?
Yes. GE Profile and Cafe series refrigerators, ranges, dishwashers, and washing machines are all fully serviced. The Cafe series uses wifi-connected controls which we work with alongside all mechanical components. Call (437) 524-1053.
My GE refrigerator is not cooling but the freezer works fine. What is the issue?
When the freezer is cold but the refrigerator is warm, the evaporator fan motor or a damper control is usually failing. This prevents cold air from circulating into the fresh food section. We diagnose and fix this same-day in most cases.
Can you repair older GE appliances from the 1990s?
Yes. GE appliance parts availability is excellent even for older machines. We service GE washers, dryers, and refrigerators regardless of age and can often repair 20-year-old units economically.
