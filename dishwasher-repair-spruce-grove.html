Common issues

Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Spruce Grove

These are the dishwasher faults we diagnose most often across Spruce Grove — concentrated in post-2014 builder-grade Whirlpool, LG, and Samsung units just timing out of warranty. Each is a same-day fix in roughly 82% of cases.

Leaking from door or base
Door gasket failure, tub crack, or sump-housing seal degradation. We pressure-test the door and replace the gasket OEM. Most calls in Greenbury and Harvest Ridge new builds.
Not starting / no power
Door latch micro-switch failure, control board fault, or thermal fuse trip. Diagnostic is straightforward — door latch replacements are 30 minutes. Common pattern in Tonewood and Copperhaven homes.
Loud noises during cycle
Wash motor bearing wear, debris in chopper assembly, or spray arm support broken. We open the unit and identify the source within 20 minutes. Routine fix in Spruce Ridge and Hilldowns.
Bosch E15 / E22 / E24 fault codes
E15 = water in base pan (leak). E22 = filter clog. E24 = drain blockage. We carry the OEM Bosch service kit on every dispatch. Frequent in Pioneer Lane and Westgrove subdivisions.
Whirlpool / KitchenAid F2 / F8 / F9 codes
F2 = control panel. F8 = water inlet flow fault. F9 = drain fault. We have the technical sheets on the truck. Pattern we see across post-2014 Spruce Grove builds.
Heating element burnout
Dishes not drying, water not heating to wash temperature. Element ohms-out at infinity = replacement. Common at 7–10 years. Most calls in Greenbury and Harvest Ridge new builds.
Not draining / standing water
Drain pump failure, clogged drain hose, or check-valve obstruction. We diagnose with a multimeter, then replace the pump or clear the line — typically a same-day fix. Common pattern in Tonewood and Copperhaven homes.
Not cleaning / dirty dishes
Spray arm scaling, circulation pump weakness, or detergent dispenser stuck. Hard Alberta water is the leading culprit — calcium scales spray nozzles within 4–6 years. Routine fix in Spruce Ridge and Hilldowns.

My Bosch dishwasher shows E15 / E22 / E24. What does that mean? +

E15 = water detected in the base pan (leak source upstream — door gasket, sump seal, or hose connection). E22 = filter clog (clean the bottom filter). E24 = drain blockage (drain hose obstruction or pump fault). E15 and E24 typically need a technician; E22 you can sometimes clear yourself with a thorough filter cleaning and a vinegar cycle. We diagnose Bosch fault codes on every Spruce Grove service call and carry the OEM Bosch service kit.
