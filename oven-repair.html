Common Oven Problems We Repair

Oven failures tend to follow recognizable patterns depending on whether the unit runs on gas or electricity. An electric oven that heats poorly often has a burned-out bake or broil element visible as a break or blister in the coil. Gas ovens that fail to ignite typically have a worn-out hot surface igniter that no longer reaches the temperature needed to open the gas valve. Temperature inaccuracies that cause burned or undercooked food point to a drifting thermostat or a failing temperature sensor probe.

  • Oven Not Heating at All: Failed bake element, blown thermal fuse, or defective igniter. Cost: $150-$300.
  • Uneven Cooking or Hot Spots: Malfunctioning convection fan motor, damaged heating element, or calibration issue. Cost: $160-$320.
  • Faulty Thermostat / Wrong Temperature: Drifting temperature sensor, worn thermostat, or control board error. Cost: $170-$350.
  • Broken or Misaligned Oven Door: Worn hinges, cracked glass, or damaged door seal allowing heat escape. Cost: $130-$280.
  • Gas Igniter Not Working: Weak hot surface igniter, faulty gas safety valve, or wiring issue. Cost: $160-$300.
  • Self-Clean Cycle Failure: Blown thermal fuse, failed door lock motor, or control board malfunction. Cost: $140-$320.

Warning Signs — Call Fixlify Right Away

Some oven problems demand immediate attention. Call Fixlify right away if you notice:

  • A gas smell when the oven is running or idle — turn off the gas valve and call immediately; this could indicate a gas leak

Oven Maintenance Tips to Prevent Costly Repairs

Regular maintenance extends your oven's lifespan and prevents the most common breakdowns we see in the Greater Toronto Area homes. Follow these five tips from our experienced technicians:

  1. Clean spills promptly — baked-on grease causes smoke, odors, and can damage the oven interior coating over time
  2. Test temperature accuracy every 6 months with an oven thermometer — a 25-degree drift is normal, but 50+ degrees means the sensor needs attention
  3. Inspect door gaskets annually — a worn rubber seal lets heat escape, increases energy costs, and causes uneven baking
  4. Avoid using the self-clean cycle more than twice a year — the extreme heat (900 F) stresses the door latch, control board, and thermal fuse
  5. For gas ovens, have igniter performance tested annually — igniters weaken gradually before failing completely
