Common issues

Common Washer Problems We Fix in Glenwood

Won't spin
Lid switch failure or motor coupling break on Whirlpool and Kenmore direct-drive top-loaders — the most common washer repair in Glenwood. Parts on the truck, typically a 45-minute fix.
Not draining
Drain pump failure or blocked filter. Front-loaders accumulate lint and hard water deposits in the pump. We clear and test the drain system on every call — often the only repair needed.
Noisy during spin
Drum bearing failure in front-loaders or worn tub bearing in top-loaders produces grinding or rumbling during spin. We diagnose the bearing condition and quote replacement before proceeding.
Front-load door seal leak
Door boot gasket tears or molds in Glenwood front-load units — accelerated by Edmonton's climate. Water leaks onto the floor. OEM gasket replacement on Samsung, LG, and Whirlpool platforms.
Washer won't start
Door lock assembly, control board, or timer faults on modern washers prevent cycles from starting. We diagnose with manufacturer tools and replace only the failed component.
Error codes
Samsung, LG, and Whirlpool front-loaders display codes for water supply, drain, and motor faults. We carry diagnostic tools to read and resolve codes on the first visit.

Glenwood washer patterns

Direct-drive top-loaders and front-load HE units — Glenwood's two washer generations

Glenwood's 1950s bungalows have laundry rooms that reflect two distinct washing machine eras. The first generation — Whirlpool and Kenmore direct-drive top-loaders from the 1990s and early 2000s — is still running in a surprising proportion of Glenwood homes. These machines are robust and repairable: motor coupling failure (the single most common repair), lid switch faults, and transmission wear are predictable, documented faults with readily available OEM parts. We see Glenwood top-loaders in the 18–25 year range regularly, and most of them have several more years of service life if the major mechanical components are sound.

The second generation consists of front-loading HE machines installed from 2010 onward. Samsung, LG, and Whirlpool front-loaders dominate this cohort, and their failure patterns are well-understood: door boot gasket deterioration by years 7–10, drain pump blockage from Edmonton's harder water supply, and bearing failure from year 10 onward in units with heavy load cycles. Both generations are well within our technicians' expertise. We advise every Glenwood homeowner on the repair economics — a $150 motor coupling replacement on a 20-year-old top-loader in good condition is almost always worth doing; a $380 drum bearing replacement on a front-loader with an otherwise clean bill of health at year 10 is typically worth doing against a $900 replacement cost.
