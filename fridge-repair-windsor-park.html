Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Windsor Park

These are the dishwasher faults we diagnose most often in Windsor Park — weighted toward premium brands like Miele, Bosch, and Sub-Zero common in estate homes, plus older heritage units in character homes.

Miele error codes (F11, F14, F78)
Miele error codes indicate drainage failure (F11), water intake fault (F14), or circulation pump issues (F78). We diagnose and repair Miele G-series and G7000 dishwashers with OEM parts same-day in Windsor Park.
Bosch E15 / E24 water fault
E15 indicates water in the base (anti-flood trigger), E24 means drain fault. Both are common in Bosch 500/800 series installed in Windsor Park renovations. We clear base flooding and fix drain pump failures efficiently.
Hard water scaling — spray arm blockage
EPCOR's 170–200 mg/L hard water leaves calcium deposits in spray arm nozzles. Premium dishwashers need recalibration of the internal water softener to prevent scaling. Common in Windsor Park units 3+ years old.
Integrated door panel misalignment
Custom panel-ready dishwashers in Windsor Park kitchen renovations can suffer hinge wear or panel bracket failure causing the custom door to sag or not close flush. We realign panels and replace worn hinges.
Circulation pump failure
Dishes come out dirty or with food residue — circulation pump is underperforming or seized. Common in older Miele and Bosch units. Pump replacement is a same-day repair for these brands when we have stock on the truck.
Control board / display failure
Electronic control board failures present as unresponsive touchpads or mid-cycle shutdowns. More common in high-end brands with complex control systems. We source OEM boards and install with full warranty.
