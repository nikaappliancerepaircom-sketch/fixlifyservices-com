Common Fridge Problems We Fix in Jamieson Place

Frequent refrigerator faults in Jamieson Place's 1980s–90s homes and renovated kitchens.

Defrost Timer

Defrost System Failure — Frigidaire

Original Frigidaire refrigerators in Jamieson Place use mechanical defrost timers that fail after 15–20 years, allowing frost to accumulate on evaporator coils. Both compartments warm, and ice forms around the back wall of the freezer. We replace the defrost timer, thermostat, and heater as a complete package to prevent recurrence.

Samsung FF

Ice Maker & Freezer Fan Faults — Samsung

Samsung French-door fridges in Jamieson Place's renovated kitchens display FF error codes from evaporator fan motor seizure or ice maker inlet valve blockage. Ice production stops and the freezer may warm slightly. We carry Samsung-compatible ice maker assemblies and fan motors for same-visit repair.

Compressor Relay

Compressor Start Relay Failure — GE

GE refrigerators from the late 1980s and early 1990s have compressor start relays that rattle and fail, preventing the compressor from starting. You'll hear clicking every few minutes or no sound at all, and the fridge warms to room temperature. We replace the relay and test compressor performance before leaving.

Door Gasket

Door Gasket Seal Failure

Door gaskets on 30-year-old Frigidaire and GE fridges have often hardened and cracked, allowing warm air infiltration that forces the compressor to run continuously. Monthly electricity bills rise noticeably. We supply period-appropriate replacement gaskets and fit them to factory tolerance, restoring the seal completely.

Evap Fan

Evaporator Fan Motor Seizure

The evaporator fan circulates cold air from the freezer into the fresh-food compartment. When it seizes — common in both older Frigidaire and newer Samsung units — both sections warm up quickly. We diagnose whether frost accumulation or motor bearing failure is the cause, replace the motor, and verify the defrost cycle.

Water Inlet

Water Inlet Valve Failure

Samsung and GE fridges with ice makers and water dispensers develop inlet valve failures that stop ice production and dispenser operation simultaneously. Mineral deposits from Edmonton's hard EPCOR water (170–200 mg/L) accelerate valve wear. We replace the valve and flush the water line as part of the repair.
