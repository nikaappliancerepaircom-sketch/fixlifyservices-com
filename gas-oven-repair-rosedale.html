Our diagnostic for temperature inconsistency in premium gas ovens involves placing three calibrated thermocouples at different rack positions and running the oven through a complete heating cycle while recording temperature data at 30-second intervals. Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Fixlify Appliance Services always test the igniter first during gas oven repair?+

The igniter is responsible for over 80 percent of gas oven no-heat complaints. It must draw 3.2 to 3.6 amps to mechanically open the safety valve through a shared electrical circuit. A weakened igniter still glows visibly but cannot generate sufficient amperage. Testing this first prevents misdiagnosis and saves homeowners hundreds of dollars on unnecessary gas valve replacements.

What is the difference between the bake and broil igniters?+

Gas ovens have two separate burner assemblies: the bake burner at the bottom of the cavity and the broil burner at the top. Each has its own dedicated igniter. A failed bake igniter means normal oven cooking will not work. A failed broil igniter only affects broiling — baking continues normally. Both igniters cost $150 to $250 to replace.

Can a gas oven produce carbon monoxide?+

Any combustion appliance can generate CO when burning fuel incompletely. A gas oven with a degraded igniter, incorrect air-fuel mixture, or a blocked internal vent can produce CO above safe levels. Fixlify Appliance Services runs a combustion analysis with a calibrated digital analyzer after every gas oven repair, measuring CO concentration and combustion efficiency. We recommend CO detectors near every gas cooking appliance.

What should I do if my gas oven smells like gas during preheating?+

A faint gas smell during the first 60 seconds of preheat can be normal as the igniter warms up. But if the smell persists, or you notice a "whump" sound when the oven finally lights, the igniter is likely degraded. It is drawing enough gas past the safety valve to accumulate before ignition.
