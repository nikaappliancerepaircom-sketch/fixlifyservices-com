GTA homeowners depend on a working washing machine every day. Across the GTA, certain repair patterns emerge based on appliance age and usage. Here are the most frequent washing machine problems our technicians encounter:

Warning Signs — Call Fixlify Right Away

Some washer problems need immediate attention to prevent water damage. Call Fixlify right away if you notice:

  • Water pooling under or around the washer — even a small leak can cause subfloor damage, mold growth, and costly structural repairs if left unchecked
  • A loud grinding or rumbling noise during spin — this typically means the drum bearing is failing and will seize if not replaced soon
  • Burning smell during operation — the motor is overheating or the drive belt is slipping and could snap, potentially damaging other components
  • The washer rocks violently or walks across the floor — failed suspension components create an unbalanced drum that can crack the outer tub
  • Visible mold or strong mildew smell from the drum or door seal — this is a health concern and indicates the boot seal needs replacement or deep cleaning

Washer Maintenance Tips to Prevent Costly Repairs

Regular maintenance extends your washing machine's lifespan and prevents the most common breakdowns we see in the Greater Toronto Area homes. Follow these five tips from our experienced technicians:

  1. Leave the washer door open after every cycle — front-loaders trap moisture in the boot seal, causing mold. Fifteen minutes of airflow prevents 90% of mold problems
  2. Use only HE (High Efficiency) detergent in front-load washers — regular detergent creates excess suds that damage the pressure sensor and leave residue on the drum bearing seal
  3. Clean the drain pump filter monthly — most front-loaders have an access panel at the bottom front. Coins, hair ties, and lint accumulate and eventually burn out the pump motor
  4. Run an empty hot water cycle with washing machine cleaner monthly — GTA water hardness causes lime scale buildup on the drum, heater element, and inlet valves
  5. Inspect water supply hoses every year and replace rubber hoses every 5 years — a burst supply hose is the most common cause of residential flooding in Ontario

Following these maintenance steps can reduce your likelihood of needing emergency washing machine repair by up to 60%.
