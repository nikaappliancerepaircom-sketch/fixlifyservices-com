How Fixlify Diagnoses Stove Problems in The Annex

When you book stove repair in The Annex through Fixlify, our TSSA-licensed technician arrives with diagnostic tools and manufacturer-specific knowledge for the Victorian semi-detached homes, many divided into apartments found in this neighbourhood.

  1. Gas safety inspection: Flex connector, shutoff valve, and burner cap seating checked before component testing. Required by Ontario TSSA regulations.

  3. Ignition system diagnostics: Spark module output, electrode alignment, and igniter amperage measured. Gas oven igniters must draw 3.0+ amps to open the safety valve.

Warning Signs — Call Fixlify Immediately in The Annex

  • Gas smell with all burners off — potential leak. Open windows, leave, call your gas utility first, then call us.
  • Yellow or orange flame instead of blue — incomplete combustion producing carbon monoxide. Immediate professional attention required.
  • Oven igniter takes 90+ seconds to light — weakening igniter approaching failure. Preventive replacement saves an emergency call.
  • Constant clicking after burners turned off — spark module short or moisture intrusion. Potential fire hazard.
  • Cracked glass cooktop with visible element — electrical shock hazard. Do not use affected zone.

Stove Maintenance Tips for The Annex

  • Clean burner caps and ignition ports monthly with a pin — food debris causes ignition delay
  • Inspect gas flex connectors annually — replace if older than 10 years
  • Use flat-bottomed cookware on glass cooktops — warped pots cause hot spots and cracks
  • Limit self-clean to once every 3-4 months — extreme heat stresses control boards
  • Keep flammable items 30 cm from stove — leading cause of kitchen fires in Canadian homes
