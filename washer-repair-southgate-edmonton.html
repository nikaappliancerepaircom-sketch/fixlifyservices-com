What We Fix

Common Washer Faults in Southgate

Frigidaire Agitator Not Moving

Worn agitator dogs or a failed drive coupling stops the agitator while the drum still spins. Common in 1990s–2000s Frigidaire and GE top-loads. Inexpensive repair with parts typically in stock.

Lid Switch Failure (Top-Load)

Many older top-loaders won't spin if the lid switch fails — a safety feature that makes the machine appear dead. A $45–$75 switch swap is usually all it takes to restore full function.

Front-Load Door Boot Seal Leak

Water pooling under front-load Whirlpool and Samsung units at 8–12 years is almost always a torn or clogged door boot seal. We replace the full seal — not just patch it — to prevent recurring leaks.

Drain Pump Obstruction or Failure

Front-load washers leaving water in the drum after the cycle typically have a blocked or failing drain pump. We clear the filter and test the pump — replacement is straightforward if needed.

Hard Water Detergent Dispenser Clog

Southgate's 175+ mg/L water builds calcium in detergent and softener compartments, causing dispensers to stick open or closed. We clean the assembly and advise on preventive descaling routines.

Drum Bearing Noise

A loud grinding or rumbling during the spin cycle usually means worn rear drum bearings. We assess whether the repair is cost-effective versus replacement — and give an honest recommendation either way.

Washer Care Tips for Southgate Edmonton Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. A neglected one typically fails at 7–9 years. Edmonton's hard water and cold winters add extra stress on washing machines in Southgate Edmonton homes — here is how to extend your washer's life based on the repairs we see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh works well in Edmonton's hard water). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration start.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Edmonton's hard water can cause slow leaks at the connection point that go undetected for months before causing floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Southgate Edmonton homes.
  • Winter tip: If your washer is in an unheated space in Southgate Edmonton, do not leave damp laundry in the drum when temperatures drop below −5°C — frozen residual water in the pump can cause pump failure on the next cycle.
