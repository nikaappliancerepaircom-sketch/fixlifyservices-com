Willowdale Gas Stove Diagnostic Approach

When a Willowdale homeowner calls Fixlify Appliance Services at (437) 524-1053 for a gas stove that is not lighting, our technician begins with the igniter electrodes. In a heavy-cooking kitchen, the spark electrode gap is typically bridged by carbonized grease — a condition that prevents the spark from jumping to the burner gas stream. We clean all electrode surfaces with a specialized abrasive pad designed for ceramic igniters, test the spark module output voltage, and verify that each burner produces a consistent blue flame pattern across all ports. If the igniter ceramic is cracked — common in kitchens where large pots are frequently dragged across the cooktop surface — we replace it with an OEM part matched to the exact range model.

For Willowdale gas oven issues (not heating, temperature inaccuracy), we follow a different diagnostic path. The oven igniter in Samsung and LG gas ranges draws between 3.2 and 3.6 amps when functioning correctly. We clamp-test the amperage at the igniter lead wire. A reading below 3.0 amps means the igniter is degraded and cannot open the oven safety valve — even though it may still glow orange and appear to be working. This is the most misdiagnosed issue in gas oven repair because the homeowner sees the igniter glowing and assumes the gas valve is the problem. Replacing the gas valve unnecessarily costs $280 to $350 — versus $150 to $200 for the actual igniter replacement. Accurate diagnosis saves Willowdale homeowners hundreds of dollars on every call.
