What We Fix

Common Washer Faults in King Edward Park

LG OE Error (Drain Failure)

OE means the LG washer can't drain within the set time. We check the pump filter first — blocked filters in Edmonton homes with hard water can fill up quickly — then test the pump. Most OE calls resolve with filter clearing or pump replacement, completed same-day.

LG LE Error (Motor Overload)

LE indicates the motor is drawing excessive current — usually because the drum is overloaded or the stator/rotor has developed a fault. We diagnose whether it's a motor component, a wiring issue, or simply a usage pattern problem before recommending parts.

LG / Samsung Door Boot Seal

Front-load door boot seals on LG and Samsung machines wear and tear at 8–12 years. Hard water mineral deposits in seal folds accelerate the process. Replacement restores a watertight seal — we use OEM LG seals to ensure correct fit.

Whirlpool Top-Load Drum Bearing

A grinding or roaring noise from a Whirlpool top-loader during spin usually means rear drum bearing failure. At 12–18 years, this is one of the most common complaints in the neighbourhood. We assess repair cost versus replacement value before proceeding.

Samsung dC / dE Door Latch Error

Samsung front-loaders that won't start and show dC or dE codes usually need a new door latch assembly — a straightforward $110–$150 repair. We carry common Samsung door latch assemblies on the van for same-day fixes.

Hard Water Detergent Dispenser

LG ThinQ dispensers are particularly narrow, making them prone to calcium blockages from Edmonton's hard water. The drawer sticks, dispenser doesn't release softener, or the drawer cracks. We clean or replace the assembly and advise on monthly descaling.

What does LG OE error mean and how do you fix it? +
OE means the LG washer detected a drainage failure — it couldn't empty within the programmed time. In King Edward Park, the most common cause is a clogged pump filter (EPCOR's hard water accelerates debris buildup). We check the filter first, then test the pump.
