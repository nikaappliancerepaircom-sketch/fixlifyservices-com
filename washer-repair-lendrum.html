What We Fix

Common Washer Faults in Lendrum

Bosch E17 / F17 Drainage Error

Bosch front-loaders reporting E17 or F17 have a drainage blockage or pump fault. The pump filter in renovated Lendrum laundry rooms often collects lint and hard water scale. We clear the filter, test the pump, and replace it if needed — usually a 60–90 minute job.

Bosch Door Boot Seal (Hard Water Damage)

Edmonton's hard water leaves mineral deposits in Bosch door seal folds, stiffening the rubber and eventually causing micro-tears that leak. We replace with OEM Bosch seals — third-party replacements often don't fit correctly on Bosch tolerances.

GE / Kenmore Transmission Failure

Older GE and Kenmore top-loaders that agitate but won't spin, or spin but won't agitate, usually have a failing transmission. We assess cost versus replacement value and give an honest recommendation before proceeding.

Bosch Drum Bearing Noise

Bosch washers at 10–15 years often develop a grinding or rumbling sound during spin cycles — worn rear drum bearings. We use OEM bearing kits and complete the job in one visit where possible. We confirm the repair is cost-effective before starting.

Hard Water Inlet Valve Scaling

Slow fill or intermittent fill failures on both Bosch and older top-loaders are frequently caused by mineral scale blocking inlet valve screens or the valve solenoid itself. We clean or replace the valve and advise on descaling intervals.

Control Board / Module Errors

Electronic control failures on Bosch front-loaders present as random error codes, mid-cycle stops, or unresponsive control panels. We diagnose the root cause — it's sometimes a wiring harness or sensor rather than the board itself — before recommending a replacement.

What does a Bosch E17 / F17 error code mean? +
E17 (or F17 on some models) indicates a drainage problem — the machine can't empty within the expected time. This is usually a clogged pump filter, a blocked drain hose, or a failing drain pump. We diagnose and fix the root cause during the initial service visit in most cases.

Washer Care Tips for Lendrum Homeowners

A well-maintained washer lasts 12–15 years. A neglected one typically fails at 7–9 years. Edmonton's hard water and cold winters add extra stress on washing machines in Lendrum homes — here is how to extend your washer's life based on the repairs we see most often:

  • Monthly: Run a hot maintenance cycle with a washer cleaner tablet (Affresh works well in Edmonton's hard water). This removes detergent residue and mineral deposits that accumulate in the drum and pump filter.
  • Monthly: Leave the front-loader door ajar between cycles to prevent mould growth. Wipe the door boot gasket with a damp cloth — this is where most front-loader odours and seal deterioration start.
  • Quarterly: Check hose connections at the back of the unit. Edmonton's hard water can cause slow leaks at the connection point that go undetected for months before causing floor damage.
  • Always: Use HE detergent in HE washers. Standard detergent over-suds in low-water machines and leaves residue that accelerates drum bearing wear — one of the most common causes of the grinding noise we repair in Lendrum homes.
  • Winter tip: If your washer is in an unheated space in Lendrum, do not leave damp laundry in the drum when temperatures drop below −5°C — frozen residual water in the pump can cause pump failure on the next cycle.
